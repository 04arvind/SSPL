General Intelligence and Reasoning Notes

Analogies

Analogies test the relationship between pairs of words or numbers.

  • Example 1: Big:Small :: Tall:Short. Big and Small are opposites, so are Tall and Short.
  • Example 2: 2:4 :: 5:10. 2 √ó 2 = 4, similarly 5 √ó 2 = 10.
  • Example 3: Doctor:Patient :: Teacher:Student. Doctor treats Patient, Teacher teaches Student.

  • Example 1: A, C, E, I, __. Pattern: +2, +2, +4. Next = I + 4 = M.
  • Example 2: 2, 4, 8, 14, 22, __. Pattern: +2, +4, +6, +8. Next = 22 + 10 = 32.
  • Example 3: Z, X, U, S, __. Pattern: -2, -3, -4. Next = S ‚Äì 5 = N.

  • Example 3: If 123 = ABC, 456 = DEF, what is 789? 789 = GHI.

Venn Diagram

Use Venn diagrams to solve problems involving overlapping sets.

  • Example 1: 50 students play cricket, 30 play football, 10 play both.
    How many play at least one? Total = 50 + 30 ‚Äì 10 = 70.
  • Example 2: 40 like tea, 25 like coffee, 15 like both.

  • Example 3: 60 speak English, 40 speak Hindi, 20 both, 10 neither.

Similarities and Differences

Identify patterns or differences in shapes, numbers, or words.

  • Example 1: Find the odd one: 1, 3, 5, 6, 7.

Analytical Reasoning

Solve problems requiring logical analysis, like seating arrangements.

  • Example 1: A, B, C sit in a row. A is on the left of B, B is on the left of C.
    Order: A, B, C.
  • Example 2: P, Q, R are in a circle. P is opposite R. Q is between P and R.
    Q faces outward.
  • Example 3: X is taller than Y but shorter than Z.
    Order: Z, X, Y.

  • Example 3: Group: Red, Blue, Green, Yellow, Black.
    Odd one out: Black (not a spectral color).
