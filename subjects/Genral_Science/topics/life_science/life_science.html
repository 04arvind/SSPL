Life Sciences (CBSE Class 10 Syllabus)

1. What is Life Sciences?

Life Sciences (Biology) studies living organisms, their structure, functions, and interactions. CBSE Class 10 covers life processes, control and coordination, reproduction, heredity, and ecosystems.

2. Key Topics

  • Life Processes: Nutrition, respiration, transportation, excretion.
  • Control and Coordination: Nervous and hormonal systems.
  • Reproduction: Asexual and sexual.
  • Heredity and Evolution: Genetics, variation, evolution.
  • Our Environment: Ecosystems, food chains.

3. Importance

Understanding life processes explains how organisms survive, reproduce, and adapt.

4. Nutrition

Definition: Obtaining and utilizing food for energy and growth.

  • Autotrophic: Plants (photosynthesis).
  • Heterotrophic: Animals (holozoic, saprophytic, parasitic).
  • Human Nutrition: Mouth, stomach, small intestine.

5. Example 1: Photosynthesis

Write the balanced equation for photosynthesis.

  • 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂.
  • Answer: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂.

6. Digestive System

  • Organs: Mouth, esophagus, stomach, intestines, liver, pancreas.
  • Enzymes: Amylase, pepsin, lipase.
  • Absorption: Villi in small intestine.

7. Example 2: Digestion

What enzyme in saliva breaks down starch?

  • Amylase (ptyalin).
  • Answer: Amylase.

8. Respiration

Definition: Breakdown of glucose for energy.

  • Aerobic: Uses O₂, mitochondria.
  • Anaerobic: No O₂, e.g., lactic acid.
  • Human: Nose, trachea, lungs (alveoli).

9. Example 3: Aerobic Respiration

How much energy is released in aerobic respiration?

  • ~32 ATP per glucose.
  • Answer: ~32 ATP.

10. Transportation

  • Plants: Xylem, phloem.
  • Humans: Heart, blood vessels, blood (RBCs, WBCs, platelets).
  • Circulation: Double (pulmonary, systemic).

11. Example 4: Blood Component

What carries oxygen in blood?

  • Hemoglobin in RBCs.
  • Answer: Hemoglobin.

12. Excretion

Definition: Removal of metabolic wastes.

  • Plants: Stomata, leaf shedding.
  • Humans: Kidneys (nephrons).
  • Dialysis: Artificial filtration.

13. Example 5: Nephron

What is the functional unit of the kidney?

  • Nephron.
  • Answer: Nephron.

14. Nervous System

  • Components: Brain, spinal cord, nerves, neurons.
  • Neuron: Dendrites, cell body, axon.
  • Reflex Action: Involuntary, spinal cord-mediated.

15. Example 6: Reflex Arc

Describe reflex action for touching a hot object.

  • Receptor → Sensory → Spinal cord → Motor → Muscle.
  • Answer: Receptor → Sensory → Spinal cord → Motor → Muscle.

16. Endocrine System

  • Glands: Pituitary, thyroid, adrenals, pancreas.
  • Hormones: Insulin, adrenaline.
  • Plant Hormones: Auxins, gibberellins.

17. Example 7: Hormone

Which hormone regulates blood sugar?

  • Insulin.
  • Answer: Insulin.

18. Reproduction

  • Asexual: Vegetative, budding.
  • Sexual: Pollination, fertilization.
  • Flower Parts: Sepals, petals, stamens, pistil.

19. Example 8: Pollination

What transfers pollen in flowers?

  • Insects, wind, water, birds.
  • Answer: Insects, wind, etc.

20. Human Reproduction

  • Male: Testes, vas deferens, penis.
  • Female: Ovaries, fallopian tubes, uterus.
  • Contraception: Barriers, hormonal, surgical.

21. Example 9: Contraception

Name a hormonal contraceptive method.

  • Oral pills.
  • Answer: Pills.

22. Heredity

  • Genes: DNA segments.
  • Mendel’s Laws: Dominance, Segregation, Independent Assortment.
  • Sex Determination: XX, XY.

23. Example 10: Monohybrid Cross

Cross Tt with Tt. Find offspring ratio.

  • 3 tall : 1 short.
  • Answer: 3:1.

24. Evolution

  • Definition: Gradual change in species.
  • Evidences: Fossils, homologous structures, vestigial organs.
  • Natural Selection: Fittest survive.

25. Example 11: Homologous Structure

Give an example of homologous structures.

  • Human and bat forelimbs.
  • Answer: Human and bat forelimbs.

26. Ecosystem

  • Components: Biotic, abiotic.
  • Food Chain: Grass → Deer → Lion.
  • Food Web: Interconnected chains.

27. Example 12: Food Chain

Construct a food chain with a hawk.

  • Grass → Mouse → Snake → Hawk.
  • Answer: Grass → Mouse → Snake → Hawk.

28. Environmental Issues

  • Biodegradable: Paper, food.
  • Non-Biodegradable: Plastics.
  • Ozone Depletion: CFCs.

29. Example 13: Biodegradable

Classify paper and plastic.

  • Paper: Biodegradable; Plastic: Non-biodegradable.
  • Answer: Paper (biodegradable), Plastic (non-biodegradable).

30. Applications

  • Agriculture: Hybrid crops.
  • Medicine: Vaccines, gene therapy.
  • Biotechnology: GMOs, DNA fingerprinting.

31. Example 14: Biotechnology

What is DNA fingerprinting used for?

  • Identification (crime, paternity).
  • Answer: Identification.
