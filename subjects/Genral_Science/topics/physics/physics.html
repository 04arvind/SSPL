Physics (CBSE Class 10 Syllabus)

1. What is Physics?

Physics is the study of natural phenomena involving matter, energy, and their interactions. The CBSE Class 10 syllabus covers light, electricity, magnetism, motion, and energy.

2. Key Topics in Class 10

  • Light: Reflection and refraction.
  • Human eye and vision.
  • Electricity: Current, circuits, and power.
  • Magnetic effects of electric current.
  • Sources of energy.

3. Importance

Physics helps understand everyday phenomena (e.g., why mirrors reflect images, how electricity powers devices).

4. Reflection of Light

Definition: Bouncing of light off a surface.

  • Laws of Reflection: Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection; rays and normal in same plane.
  • Plane Mirror: Virtual, upright, same size, laterally inverted image.

5. Example 1: Plane Mirror

An object is placed 5 cm in front of a plane mirror. Find the image distance.

  • Image distance = 5 cm behind the mirror.
  • Answer: 5 cm.

6. Refraction of Light

Definition: Bending of light when passing between media.

  • Laws of Refraction: Snell’s Law (sin i / sin r = n); rays and normal in same plane.
  • Refractive Index: n = c/v.

7. Example 2: Refraction

Light enters glass (n = 1.5) from air at 30°. Find angle of refraction.

  • sin r = 0.5 / 1.5 = 0.333; r ≈ 19.5°.
  • Answer: 19.5°.

8. Lenses

  • Convex Lens: Converging; real/virtual images.
  • Concave Lens: Diverging; virtual images.
  • Lens Formula: 1/f = 1/v – 1/u.
  • Power: P = 1/f (dioptre).

9. Example 3: Convex Lens

A convex lens (f = 20 cm) has an object at 30 cm. Find image distance.

  • 1/v = 1/20 + 1/30; v = 12 cm.
  • Answer: 12 cm.

10. Human Eye

  • Parts: Cornea, iris, pupil, lens, retina.
  • Accommodation: Lens adjusts focus.
  • Defects: Myopia (concave lens), Hypermetropia (convex lens), Presbyopia (bifocal).

11. Example 4: Myopia

A person’s far point is 2 m. Find power of corrective lens.

  • P = 1/(–2) = –0.5 D.
  • Answer: –0.5 D.

12. Electric Current

  • Definition: I = Q/t (ampere).
  • Ohm’s Law: V = IR.
  • Resistance: R = ρL/A.

13. Example 5: Ohm’s Law

A 12 V battery is connected to a 4 Ω resistor. Find current.

  • I = 12/4 = 3 A.
  • Answer: 3 A.

14. Electric Circuits

  • Series: R_total = R1 + R2; same current.
  • Parallel: 1/R_total = 1/R1 + 1/R2; same voltage.
  • Power: P = VI.

15. Example 6: Parallel Circuit

Two resistors (6 Ω, 12 Ω) are in parallel. Find equivalent resistance.

  • 1/R_total = 1/6 + 1/12; R_total = 4 Ω.
  • Answer: 4 Ω.

16. Magnetic Field

  • Definition: Region of magnetic forces.
  • Field Lines: N to S, no intersection.
  • Right-Hand Rule: Thumb = current, fingers = field.

17. Example 7: Magnetic Force

A 2 A current flows in a 0.1 m wire in 0.5 T field (θ = 90°). Find force.

  • F = 0.5 × 2 × 0.1 = 0.1 N.
  • Answer: 0.1 N.

18. Sources of Energy

  • Renewable: Solar, wind, hydro, biomass.
  • Non-Renewable: Coal, oil, gas, nuclear.
  • Solar Energy: Photovoltaic cells.

19. Example 8: Solar Power

A solar panel generates 200 W for 5 hours. Find energy in kWh.

  • Energy = 200 × 5 = 1000 Wh = 1 kWh.
  • Answer: 1 kWh.

20. Practice Problems

  1. An object is 10 cm from a plane mirror. Find image distance.
  2. Find angle of refraction for light entering water (n = 1.33) at 45°.
  3. A concave lens has f = –15 cm. Find image distance for object at 30 cm.
  4. Calculate power of a lens with f = 50 cm.
  5. Find current through a 10 Ω resistor with 20 V.
  6. Two resistors (3 Ω, 6 Ω) in series. Find equivalent resistance.
  7. Find force on a 0.2 m wire with 3 A in 0.4 T field (θ = 90°).
  8. A wind turbine generates 500 W for 8 hours. Find energy in kWh.
  9. A convex lens forms a real image at 40 cm for object at 20 cm. Find f.
  10. A person’s near point is 50 cm. Find power of corrective lens for hypermetropia.

21. Solutions

  1. 10 cm.
  2. r ≈ 32°.
  3. v = –10 cm.
  4. P = 2 D.
  5. I = 2 A.
  6. R_total = 9 Ω.
  7. F = 0.24 N.
  8. 4 kWh.
  9. f = 40/3 cm.
  10. P = 2 D.

22. Tips

  • Draw ray diagrams for light and lenses.
  • Memorize Ohm’s Law and circuit formulas.
  • Practice magnetic field rules with diagrams.
  • Understand renewable vs. non-renewable energy.
