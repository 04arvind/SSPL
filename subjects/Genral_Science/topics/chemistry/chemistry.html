Chemistry (CBSE Class 10 Syllabus)

1. What is Chemistry?

Chemistry studies the composition, properties, and reactions of matter. The CBSE Class 10 syllabus covers chemical reactions, acids and bases, metals and non-metals, carbon compounds, and periodic classification.

2. Key Topics in Class 10

  • Chemical reactions and equations.
  • Acids, bases, and salts.
  • Metals and non-metals.
  • Carbon and its compounds.
  • Periodic classification of elements.

3. Importance

Chemistry explains processes like digestion, corrosion, and fuel combustion.

4. Chemical Reactions

Definition: Process where reactants form products.

  • Types: Combination, Decomposition, Displacement, Double Displacement.
  • Balancing: Equal atoms on both sides.

5. Example 1: Balancing

Balance: Fe + O₂ → Fe₂O₃.

  • Balanced: 4Fe + 3O₂ → 2Fe₂O₃.
  • Answer: 4Fe + 3O₂ → 2Fe₂O₃.

6. Acids and Bases

  • Acids: Sour, H⁺ ions, blue litmus red.
  • Bases: Bitter, OH⁻ ions, red litmus blue.
  • pH Scale: 0 (acidic) to 14 (basic).
  • Neutralization: Acid + Base → Salt + Water.

7. Example 2: Neutralization

Write the reaction for HCl + NaOH.

  • HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O.
  • Answer: NaCl + H₂O.

8. Salts

  • Definition: From neutralization.
  • Types: Neutral, Acidic, Basic.
  • Uses: NaCl (cooking), NaHCO₃ (baking).

9. Example 3: Salt Formation

Identify the salt from H₂SO₄ + KOH.

  • H₂SO₄ + 2KOH → K₂SO₄ + 2H₂O.
  • Answer: K₂SO₄.

10. Metals and Non-Metals

  • Metals: Shiny, conductive, malleable.
  • Non-Metals: Dull, poor conductors.
  • Reactivity Series: K > Na > Ca > … > Au.
  • Corrosion: Metal oxidation (e.g., rust).

11. Example 4: Reactivity

Will Zn displace Cu from CuSO₄?

  • Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu.
  • Answer: Yes.

12. Carbon and Its Compounds

  • Catenation: Forms chains/rings.
  • Tetravalency: 4 covalent bonds.
  • Hydrocarbons: Alkanes, Alkenes, Alkynes.
  • Functional Groups: OH, COOH.

13. Example 5: Hydrocarbon

Name C₃H₈ and its type.

  • C₃H₈ = Propane, alkane.
  • Answer: Propane, alkane.

14. Soaps and Detergents

  • Soap: Fatty acid salts, soft water.
  • Detergent: Synthetic, hard water.
  • Cleansing: Micelles trap dirt.

15. Example 6: Soap Reaction

Write the saponification reaction.

  • Fat + NaOH → Soap + Glycerol.
  • Answer: Soap + Glycerol.

16. Periodic Table

  • Mendeleev: By atomic mass.
  • Modern: By atomic number.
  • Trends: Atomic size, metallic character, valency.

17. Example 7: Periodic Trend

Compare atomic sizes of Na and Cl (Period 3).

  • Na > Cl (size decreases across period).
  • Answer: Na > Cl.

18. Exothermic and Endothermic Reactions

  • Exothermic: Release heat.
  • Endothermic: Absorb heat.
  • Redox: Oxidation + Reduction.

19. Example 8: Redox

Identify oxidation in 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO.

  • Mg → Mg²⁺ (oxidized).
  • Answer: Mg is oxidized.

20. Practice Problems

  1. Balance: Al + O₂ → Al₂O₃.
  2. Write reaction for HNO₃ + Ca(OH)₂.
  3. Identify salt from HCl + NH₄OH.
  4. Will Mg displace Fe from FeSO₄?
  5. Name C₄H₁₀ and its type.
  6. Write reaction for ethanol combustion.
  7. Compare electronegativity of F and O (Period 2).
  8. Identify reduction in CuO + H₂ → Cu + H₂O.
  9. What is pH of NaCl solution?
  10. Write structure of ethene (C₂H₄).

21. Solutions

  1. 4Al + 3O₂ → 2Al₂O₃.
  2. 2HNO₃ + Ca(OH)₂ → Ca(NO₃)₂ + 2H₂O.
  3. NH₄Cl.
  4. Yes.
  5. Butane, alkane.
  6. C₂H₅OH + 3O₂ → 2CO₂ + 3H₂O.
  7. F > O.
  8. Cu²⁺ → Cu.
  9. pH ≈ 7.
  10. H₂C=CH₂.

22. Tips

  • Practice balancing equations.
  • Memorize reactivity series and functional groups.
  • Understand periodic trends with examples.
  • Draw structures for carbon compounds.
