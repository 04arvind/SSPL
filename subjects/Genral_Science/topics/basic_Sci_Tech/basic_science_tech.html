Basic Concepts of Science and Technology

1. What is Science and Technology?

Science studies the natural world; technology applies science for practical use. CBSE Class 10 covers mechanics, energy, communication, biotechnology, and space tech.

2. Key Areas

  • Mechanics: Force, work, energy, machines.
  • Energy: Forms, conservation.
  • Communication: Radio, TV, internet.
  • Biotechnology: Health, agriculture.
  • Space Technology: Satellites, ISRO.

3. Importance

Drives innovation in healthcare, agriculture, communication.

4. Force

Definition: Push/pull changing motion (Newton).

  • Types: Contact, non-contact.
  • Newton’s Laws: Inertia, F=ma, action=reaction.

5. Example 1: Newton’s 2nd Law

A 5 kg object accelerates at 2 m/s². Find force.

  • F = 5 × 2 = 10 N.
  • Answer: 10 N.

6. Work

Definition: Force × Distance (Joule).

  • Condition: Same direction.
  • Power: Work/Time (Watt).

7. Example 2: Work

A 20 N force moves 5 m. Find work.

  • W = 20 × 5 = 100 J.
  • Answer: 100 J.

8. Energy

  • Forms: Kinetic, potential, thermal.
  • Conservation: Cannot be created/destroyed.
  • Renewable: Solar, wind.
  • Non-Renewable: Coal, oil.

9. Example 3: Kinetic Energy

A 2 kg object moves at 3 m/s. Find KE.

  • KE = ½ × 2 × 3² = 9 J.
  • Answer: 9 J.

10. Simple Machines

  • Types: Lever, pulley, inclined plane.
  • MA: Load/Effort.
  • Efficiency: Output/Input × 100%.

11. Example 4: Lever

A lever lifts 200 N with 50 N effort. Find MA.

  • MA = 200/50 = 4.
  • Answer: 4.

12. Communication
