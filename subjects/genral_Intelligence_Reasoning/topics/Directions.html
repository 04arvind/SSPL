Direction and Distance - General Intelligence and Reasoning

1. Introduction to Direction and Distance

Direction and Distance is a reasoning topic that involves determining the position, direction, or distance of an object or person based on given movements or conditions. It tests spatial awareness, logical deduction, and the ability to interpret directional instructions. This topic is common in competitive exams to assess navigation and problem-solving skills.

Objective: Calculate the final position, direction, or distance after a series of movements or determine relationships between points.

Applications: Used in navigation, logistics, and geographical analysis.

2. Types of Direction and Distance Questions

Questions can be categorized as follows:

  • Linear Movement: Movements along a straight line (e.g., north, south).
  • Two-Dimensional Movement: Movements in a plane using cardinal directions (e.g., north, east, south, west).
  • Angular Movement: Movements with specific angles (e.g., 90° turns).
  • Shortest Distance: Calculate the shortest path between two points.
  • Relative Position: Determine the position of one point relative to another.
  • Coded Directions: Directions given in coded form (e.g., left = west).

3. Key Concepts

Understanding these concepts is essential:

  • Cardinal Directions: North, East, South, West (N, E, S, W).
  • Intermediate Directions: Northeast (NE), Northwest (NW), Southeast (SE), Southwest (SW).
  • Turns: Left (anticlockwise) or right (clockwise) by specific angles (e.g., 90°, 180°).
  • Distance Calculation: Use Pythagorean theorem for shortest distance: √(x² + y²).
  • Coordinate System: Represent positions as (x, y) for precise tracking.

4. Steps to Solve Direction and Distance Questions

  1. Identify the starting point and initial direction (if given).
  2. Track movements using a coordinate system or diagram.
  3. Update position after each movement (e.g., North +5 → y+5).
  4. Calculate final position or distance using coordinates or Pythagorean theorem.
  5. For direction questions, determine the final facing direction after turns.
  6. Verify by retracing steps or checking against conditions.

Example 1 (Two-Dimensional Movement): A person starts at point O, walks 4 km North, then 3 km East. What is the shortest distance from O?

Solution:

  • Start: O (0,0).
  • North 4 km → (0,4).
  • East 3 km → (3,4).
  • Shortest distance = √(3² + 4²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5 km.
  • Answer: 5 km.

N | 4 | (3,4) |---- E O(0,0) 3

Example 2 (Angular Movement): A person faces North, turns 90° right, walks 5 km, turns 180°, walks 5 km. What direction is he facing?

Solution:

  • Start: Facing North.
  • 90° right → Facing East.
  • Walk 5 km → No change in direction.
  • 180° turn → East to West.
  • Walk 5 km → No change in direction.
  • Answer: West.

Example 3 (Shortest Distance): A person walks 6 km West, 8 km North. What is the shortest distance to the starting point?

Solution:

  • Start: (0,0).
  • West 6 km → (-6,0).
  • North 8 km → (-6,8).
  • Shortest distance = √((-6)² + 8²) = √(36 + 64) = √100 = 10 km.
  • Answer: 10 km.

N | 8 | (-6,8) |---- E O(0,0) -6 W

5. Practice Questions

Question 1: A person walks 5 km South, then 12 km East. What is the shortest distance from the starting point?

A) 13 km B) 14 km C) 15 km D) 17 km

Solution:

  • Start: (0,0).
  • South 5 km → (0,-5).
  • East 12 km → (12,-5).
  • Distance = √(12² + (-5)²) = √(144 + 25) = √169 = 13 km.
  • Answer: A.

Question 2: A person faces East, turns 90° left, walks 3 km, turns 90° left, walks 4 km. What direction is he facing?

A) North B) East C) South D) West

Solution:

  • Start: Facing East.
  • 90° left → North.
  • Walk 3 km → No change.
  • 90° left → West.
  • Walk 4 km → No change.
  • Answer: D) West.

Question 3: A person walks 3 km North, 4 km West, 3 km South. Where is he relative to the starting point?

A) 4 km West B) 4 km East C) 3 km West D) At starting point

Solution:

  • Start: (0,0).
  • North 3 km → (0,3).
  • West 4 km → (-4,3).
  • South 3 km → (-4,0).
  • Final: 4 km West of starting point.
  • Answer: A.

6. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Misinterpreting directions (e.g., confusing left with right turns).
  • Forgetting to account for opposite movements (e.g., North 5 km, South 5 km = 0).
  • Incorrectly applying Pythagorean theorem for shortest distance.
  • Neglecting final direction after multiple turns.
  • Ignoring coordinate system for complex movements.

7. Tips for Preparation

  • Practice drawing coordinate diagrams for movements.
  • Memorize direction changes for 90°, 180°, and 270° turns.
  • Solve 20–30 direction and distance questions daily.
  • Use Pythagorean theorem for shortest distance calculations.
  • Verify final position and direction by retracing steps.

9. Advanced Direction and Distance Questions

Advanced questions involve multiple turns, coded directions, or complex paths.

Example (Coded Directions): In a code, Left = North, Right = South, Forward = East, Backward = West. A person faces Forward, moves Right 5 km, Left 5 km, Backward 3 km. What is the shortest distance from start?

Solution:

  • Code: Left = North, Right = South, Forward = East, Backward = West.
  • Start: Facing East.
  • Right 5 km → South 5 km → (0,-5).
  • Left 5 km → North 5 km → (0,0).
  • Backward 3 km → West 3 km → (-3,0).
  • Distance = √((-3)² + 0²) = 3 km.
  • Answer: 3 km.

10. Practice Set (10 Questions)

Additional questions to reinforce learning:

Question 1: A person walks 8 km North, 6 km West, 8 km South. Where is he relative to the start?

A) 6 km West B) 6 km East C) 8 km West D) At start

Solution: (0,0) → (0,8) → (-6,8) → (-6,0). Answer: A.

Question 2: Ravi walks 10 m North, turns right, walks 5 m, turns right again and walks 10 m. How far is he from the starting point?

A) 10 m B) 5 m C) 15 m D) 0 m

Solution: Ends 5 m East of start. Answer: B.

Question 3: A man moves 3 km East, then 4 km North. How far is he from the start?

A) 5 km B) 7 km C) 1 km D) 6 km

Solution: √(3² + 4²) = 5 km. Answer: A.

Question 4: A girl goes 5 km South, turns left, walks 4 km, then turns left again and walks 5 km. Where is she from the start?

A) 4 km West B) 5 km East C) 9 km South D) At start

Solution: Back in line with start, 4 km West. Answer: A.

Question 5: If you face North and turn 270° clockwise, which direction will you face?

A) East B) South C) West D) North

Solution: North → East → South → West. Answer: C.

Question 6: Rohit walks 12 km North, 5 km East, then 12 km South. Distance from start?

A) 5 km B) 12 km C) 0 km D) 24 km

Solution: Ends 5 km East. Answer: A.

Question 7: A man walks 7 km West, then 5 km South, then 7 km East. How far and in which direction is he from the start?

A) 5 km South B) 5 km North C) 7 km West D) 7 km East

Solution: Ends 5 km South. Answer: A.

Question 8: Facing South, if you turn 135° clockwise, which direction are you facing?

A) South-West B) West C) North-West D) East

Solution: South → West (90°) → North-West (45° more). Answer: C.

Question 9: A boy starts North, walks 6 km, turns right, walks 8 km, then left and walks 6 km. Final direction?

A) East B) West C) North D) South

Solution: Ends moving North → East → North. Answer: C.

Question 10: A woman walks 10 m North, 6 m East, 4 m South, then 6 m West. Distance from starting point?

A) 6 m B) 4 m C) 10 m D) 16 m

Solution: Net movement = 6 m N, 0 E-W. Answer: B.
