Analogies - General Intelligence and Reasoning

1. Introduction to Analogies

An analogy is a comparison between two pairs of words or concepts that share a similar relationship. In reasoning, analogies test your ability to identify and apply relationships between words or numbers. Solving analogies enhances logical thinking and pattern recognition.

Format: A : B :: C : D (A is to B as C is to D).

Objective: Find the word that completes the analogy by identifying the relationship between A and B, then applying it to C and D.

2. Types of Analogies

Analogies can be classified based on the relationship between the words. Common types include:

  • Synonym Analogy: Words are similar in meaning (e.g., Big : Large :: Small : Tiny).
  • Antonym Analogy: Words are opposites (e.g., Big : Small :: Tall : Short).
  • Part-Whole Analogy: One word is a part of the other (e.g., Finger : Hand :: Leaf : Tree).
  • Function Analogy: One word describes the function of the other (e.g., Pen : Write :: Scissors : Cut).
  • Degree Analogy: Difference in intensity (e.g., Warm : Hot :: Cool : Cold).
  • Cause-Effect Analogy: One causes the other (e.g., Fire : Heat :: Rain : Wet).
  • Number Analogy: Based on numerical patterns (e.g., 2 : 4 :: 3 : 9).

3. Steps to Solve Analogies

  1. Identify the relationship between the first pair (A : B).
  2. Apply the same relationship to the second pair (C : D).
  3. Choose the word that completes the analogy.
  4. Verify the relationship for consistency.

Example 1: Cat : Kitten :: Dog : ?

Solution: Cat is the adult, and Kitten is its young. Similarly, Dog is the adult, and Puppy is its young. Answer: Puppy.

Example 2: 3 : 9 :: 5 : ?

Solution: 3 × 3 = 9. Similarly, 5 × 5 = 25. Answer: 25.

4. Practice Questions

Question 1: Big : Small :: Heavy : ?

A) Light B) Large C) Tiny D) Strong

Answer: A) Light (Big and Small are opposites, so Heavy’s opposite is Light).

Question 2: Pen : Paper :: Hammer : ?

A) Nail B) Wood C) Screw D) Wall

Answer: A) Nail (Pen is used on Paper; Hammer is used on Nail).

Question 3: 4 : 16 :: 6 : ?

A) 24 B) 36 C) 18 D) 12

Answer: B) 36 (4² = 16; 6² = 36).

5. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Misidentifying the relationship (e.g., confusing synonym with function).
  • Ignoring the order of words (A : B is not the same as B : A).
  • Choosing distractors that seem related but don’t fit the exact relationship.

6. Tips for Preparation

  • Build vocabulary to recognize synonyms, antonyms, and related words.
  • Practice number patterns for numerical analogies.
  • Solve at least 20 analogies daily to improve speed and accuracy.
  • Use elimination to narrow down answer choices.

8. Advanced Analogies

For competitive exams, analogies may involve complex relationships or abstract concepts.

Example: Symphony : Composer :: Painting : ?

Solution: A symphony is created by a composer; a painting is created by a painter. Answer: Painter.
