Economics Notes (General Awareness 2025)

1. Introduction to Economics

Economics studies the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services. For competitive exams (UPSC, SSC, CBSE Class 10), Economics covers basic concepts (micro/macroeconomics), Indian economy (planning, sectors, reforms), and global trends. It accounts for 8–12 questions in General Awareness, testing concepts, policies, and current affairs (2024–2025).

Importance: Economics reflects national development, resource allocation, and global trade, critical for understanding policy and progress.

2. Basic Economic Concepts

2.1 Microeconomics

  • Demand and Supply: Demand (quantity buyers want), supply (quantity sellers offer); equilibrium price balances both.
  • Elasticity: Sensitivity of demand/supply to price changes (e.g., elastic for luxury goods).
  • Market Structures: Perfect competition, monopoly, oligopoly, monopolistic competition.

Example 1: What determines equilibrium price?

Intersection of demand and supply. Answer: Demand and supply.

2.2 Macroeconomics

  • GDP: Gross Domestic Product; total value of goods/services produced (Nominal vs. Real).
  • Inflation: Rise in price levels; measured by CPI (Consumer Price Index), WPI.
  • Unemployment: Types (frictional, structural, cyclical); measured by Labour Force Survey.
  • Fiscal Policy: Government spending/taxation to influence economy.
  • Monetary Policy: RBI’s control of money supply (e.g., repo rate).

Example 2: What measures the general price rise?

Consumer Price Index (CPI). Answer: CPI.

3. Indian Economy: Historical Overview

  • Pre-Independence: Colonial exploitation; agriculture-based; drain of wealth.
  • Post-1947: Mixed economy; Five-Year Plans (1951 onward); focus on self-reliance.
  • 1991 Reforms: Liberalization, privatization, globalization (LPG); opened economy to FDI.

Example 3: What triggered India’s 1991 economic reforms?

Balance of Payments crisis. Answer: BoP crisis.

4.1 Agriculture

  • Crops: Rice, wheat, pulses, sugarcane.
  • Revolutions: Green (1960s, foodgrains), White (milk), Blue (fish).
  • Schemes: PM-KISAN, Crop Insurance (PMFBY).

4.2 Industry

  • Segments: Manufacturing, mining, construction.
  • Initiatives: Make in India, PLI (Production-Linked Incentive).
  • PSUs: ONGC, SAIL, NTPC.

4.3 Services

  • Segments: IT, banking, tourism.
  • IT Sector: Bengaluru, Hyderabad hubs; $250 billion export (2024).
  • Initiatives: Digital India, UPI.

Example 4: Which sector employs the most in India?

Agriculture (~45%). Answer: Agriculture.

5. Economic Planning in India

  • Five-Year Plans: 1951–2017; focused on growth, poverty alleviation.
  • NITI Aayog: Replaced Planning Commission (2015); cooperative federalism.
  • Goals: $5 trillion economy by 2025 (delayed to 2027); Viksit Bharat@2047.

Example 5: What replaced the Planning Commission?

NITI Aayog (2015). Answer: NITI Aayog.

6. Indian Budget and Taxation

  • Union Budget: Annual financial statement (Article 112); presented February 1.
  • Budget 2024–25: ₹48.21 lakh crore; focus on infra, jobs, green energy.
  • Taxes: Direct (income, corporate), Indirect (GST, introduced 2017).
  • Deficits: Fiscal (4.9% of GDP, 2024), Revenue, Primary.

Example 6: When is the Union Budget presented?

February 1. Answer: February 1.

7. Banking and Financial Institutions

  • RBI: Established 1935; controls monetary policy; Governor (2025): Shaktikanta Das.
  • Banks: Public (SBI), Private (HDFC), Regional Rural Banks.
  • Financial Bodies: SEBI (stock markets), IRDAI (insurance), PFRDA (pension).
  • Digital Payments: UPI (₹20 lakh crore monthly transactions, 2024).

Example 7: Who regulates India’s stock markets?

SEBI. Answer: SEBI.

8. Foreign Trade and FDI

  • Exports: $776 billion (2023–24); gems, petroleum, IT services.
  • Imports: $854 billion; crude oil, electronics, gold.
  • Trade Deficit: $78 billion (2023–24).
  • FDI: $85 billion (2023–24); Singapore, USA top sources.
  • Initiatives: Atmanirbhar Bharat, FTAs with UAE, Australia (2022).

Example 8: What is India’s top export item?

Gems and jewellery. Answer: Gems and jewellery.

9. Poverty and Unemployment

  • Poverty: 11.9% below poverty line (2022–23, NITI Aayog); multidimensional poverty index.
  • Unemployment: 7.1% (2023–24, CMIE); youth unemployment a concern.
  • Schemes: MGNREGA (100 days rural work), PMEGP (employment generation).

Example 9: What provides 100 days of rural employment?

MGNREGA. Answer: MGNREGA.

10. Economic Reforms in India

  • 1991 LPG: Deregulation, FDI, privatization.
  • GST (2017): Unified tax; 5%, 12%, 18%, 28% slabs.
  • Insolvency Code (2016): Bankruptcy resolution; improved ease of doing business.
  • Recent (2024): Labour codes, green energy push.

Example 10: What unified India’s indirect taxes?

GST (2017). Answer: GST.

11. Global Economy

  • IMF/World Bank: IMF forecasts global growth at 3.2% (2025).
  • Top Economies: USA ($25 trillion), China ($18 trillion), India ($3.9 trillion, 5th, 2024).
  • Trade Organizations: WTO, G20, BRICS.
  • Challenges: Inflation, geopolitical tensions, climate costs.

Example 11: Which country has the largest GDP?

USA. Answer: USA.

12. Indian Economy: Recent Developments (2024–2025)

  • GDP Growth: 6.7% (2024–25, RBI estimate); India 3rd largest by 2027 (IMF).
  • Budget 2024–25: ₹2 lakh crore for jobs; 9 priorities (infra, MSMEs, green tech).
  • PLI Scheme: ₹2 lakh crore allocated; boosts electronics, solar.
  • Inflation: CPI at 5.5% (2024); food inflation a concern.
  • Stock Market: Sensex at 80,000 (2024); 4th largest globally.

Example 12: What is India’s GDP growth estimate for 2024–25?

6.7% (RBI). Answer: 6.7%.

13. Sustainable Development

  • SDGs: 17 UN goals (2030); India ranks 66th in SDG Index (2024).
  • Green Energy: 200 GW renewable capacity (2024); target 500 GW by 2030.
  • Initiatives: National Green Hydrogen Mission, PM Suryodaya Yojana.

Example 13: What is India’s renewable energy target for 2030?

500 GW. Answer: 500 GW.

14. Economic Institutions in India

  • Finance Ministry: Oversees budget; FM (2025): Nirmala Sitharaman.
  • NITI Aayog: Policy think-tank; CEO: BVR Subrahmanyam.
  • CSO: Central Statistics Office; GDP, inflation data.
  • NSO: National Statistical Office; conducts surveys.

Example 14: Who is India’s Finance Minister in 2025?

Nirmala Sitharaman. Answer: Nirmala Sitharaman.

16. Economic Challenges

  • Inequality: Top 1% hold 40% wealth (2024, Oxfam).
  • Jobless Growth: High GDP, low job creation.
  • Climate Impact: Floods, droughts affect agriculture.
  • Global Risks: Oil price volatility, trade wars.

Example 16: What is a key economic challenge in India?

Income inequality. Answer: Inequality.

17. Women in Indian Economy

  • Participation: Female LFPR 37% (2023–24); rural higher than urban.
  • Schemes: Beti Bachao Beti Padhao, Mudra Yojana (50% women beneficiaries).
  • Leaders: Nirmala Sitharaman (FM), Indra Nooyi (ex-PepsiCo CEO).

Example 17: What scheme promotes women entrepreneurship?

Mudra Yojana. Answer: Mudra Yojana.

18. Current Economic Affairs (2024–2025)

  • Budget 2024–25: ₹10 lakh crore for infra; 4 crore jobs target.
  • Repo Rate: 6.5% (RBI, 2024); unchanged to control inflation.
  • FTA: India-UK talks ongoing; EFTA signed (2024).
  • Cryptocurrency: RBI’s digital rupee pilot expands (2024).
  • Global Rank: India 3rd in PPP (2024, IMF).
