Important Schemes and Policies Notes (General Awareness 2025)

1. Introduction to Schemes and Policies

Important Schemes and Policies refer to Indian government initiatives aimed at socio-economic development, welfare, and governance. For competitive exams (UPSC, SSC, CBSE Class 10), this topic covers flagship programs, their objectives, and updates (2014–2025), accounting for 8–12 questions in General Awareness. Key areas include housing, health, education, agriculture, and digitalization.

Importance: These schemes reflect India’s policy priorities, impacting millions and shaping exam syllabi.

2. Housing and Urban Development

2.1 Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY)

  • Launched: June 2015.
  • Objective: Housing for all by 2024 (urban, rural).
  • Components: PMAY-Urban (1.12 crore houses), PMAY-Gramin (2.95 crore houses).
  • Update 2024: Extended to 2025; 3 crore more houses sanctioned.

Example 1: What is the goal of PMAY?

Housing for all. Answer: Housing for all.

2.2 Smart Cities Mission

  • Launched: June 2015.
  • Objective: Develop 100 sustainable, tech-driven cities.
  • Features: Smart mobility, energy, governance.
  • Update 2024: 80% projects completed; Varanasi, Surat lead.

Example 2: How many cities are under Smart Cities Mission?

100. Answer: 100.

3. Health and Sanitation

3.1 Ayushman Bharat

  • Launched: September 2018.
  • Components: PM-JAY (health insurance), Health & Wellness Centres.
  • Objective: Rs. 5 lakh coverage for 50 crore people; 1.5 lakh HWCs.
  • Update 2024: 25 crore beneficiaries; senior citizens (70+) covered.

Example 3: What is the insurance coverage under PM-JAY?

Rs. 5 lakh. Answer: Rs. 5 lakh.

3.2 Swachh Bharat Mission

  • Launched: October 2014.
  • Objective: Open defecation-free (ODF) India; waste management.
  • Achievements: 11 crore toilets built; ODF status (2019).
  • Update 2024: SBM 2.0; focus on plastic waste, urban sanitation.

Example 4: When was Swachh Bharat Mission launched?

October 2014. Answer: October 2014.

4. Education and Skill Development

4.1 National Education Policy (NEP) 2020

  • Announced: July 2020.
  • Objective: Holistic education; 5+3+3+4 structure.
  • Features: Multilingualism, vocational training, 6% GDP on education.
  • Update 2024: 4-year UG courses; 90% schools aligned.

Example 5: What is NEP 2020’s school structure?

5+3+3+4. Answer: 5+3+3+4.

4.2 Skill India Mission

  • Launched: July 2015.
  • Objective: Train 40 crore people by 2022; extended to 2025.
  • Programs: PMKVY, NSDC, ITIs.
  • Update 2024: 1.4 crore trained; focus on green jobs.

Example 6: What is the flagship program of Skill India?

PMKVY. Answer: PMKVY.

5. Agriculture and Rural Development

5.1 PM Kisan Samman Nidhi

  • Launched: February 2019.
  • Objective: Rs. 6,000/year to small farmers (2 hectares or less).
  • Beneficiaries: 12 crore farmers.
  • Update 2024: 18th installment released; Rs. 2 lakh crore disbursed.

Example 7: What is the annual aid under PM Kisan?

Rs. 6,000. Answer: Rs. 6,000.

5.2 PM Fasal Bima Yojana

  • Launched: January 2016.
  • Objective: Crop insurance against natural calamities.
  • Features: Low premium (2% Kharif, 1.5% Rabi).
  • Update 2024: 6 crore farmers insured; tech-driven claims.

Example 8: What is the premium for Kharif under PMFBY?

2%. Answer: 2%.

6. Digital and Financial Inclusion

6.1 Digital India

  • Launched: July 2015.
  • Objective: Digital empowerment; e-governance, connectivity.
  • Components: BharatNet, DigiLocker, UPI.
  • Update 2024: UPI at 16 billion transactions/month; 5G in 98% urban areas.

Example 9: What is a component of Digital India?

UPI. Answer: UPI.

6.2 Jan Dhan Yojana

  • Launched: August 2014.
  • Objective: Bank accounts for all; financial inclusion.
  • Features: Zero-balance accounts, RuPay card, overdraft.
  • Update 2024: 53 crore accounts; 67% women account holders.

Example 10: What card is issued under Jan Dhan?

RuPay. Answer: RuPay.

7. Infrastructure and Connectivity

7.1 Bharatmala Pariyojana

  • Launched: 2017.
  • Objective: 83,677 km of highways; economic corridors.
  • Features: NH development, border roads.
  • Update 2024: 35,000 km completed; Rs. 5.35 lakh crore invested.

Example 11: What is the focus of Bharatmala?

Highways. Answer: Highways.

7.2 Sagarmala Project

  • Launched: 2015.
  • Objective: Port-led development; 14,500 km coastline.
  • Features: Port modernization, coastal economic zones.
  • Update 2024: 800 projects; Mumbai Coastal Road completed.

Example 12: What does Sagarmala modernize?

Ports. Answer: Ports.

8. Women and Child Welfare

8.1 Beti Bachao Beti Padhao

  • Launched: January 2015.
  • Objective: Improve sex ratio; educate girls.
  • Impact: Sex ratio at birth improved to 933 (2024).
  • Update 2024: Expanded to STEM education for girls.

Example 13: What is the aim of Beti Bachao Beti Padhao?

Improve sex ratio. Answer: Improve sex ratio.

8.2 PM Matru Vandana Yojana

  • Launched: 2017.
  • Objective: Rs. 5,000 aid for pregnant women.
  • Beneficiaries: 3 crore women (2024).
  • Update 2024: Linked to nutrition programs.

Example 14: What aid does PMMVY provide?

Rs. 5,000. Answer: Rs. 5,000.

9. Tribal and Marginalized Groups

9.1 PM JANMAN

  • Launched: November 2023.
  • Objective: Uplift Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs).
  • Features: Housing, education, health for 75 PVTGs.
  • Update 2024: Rs. 24,000 crore allocated; 5 lakh beneficiaries.

Example 15: Who benefits from PM JANMAN?

PVTGs. Answer: PVTGs.

9.2 Eklavya Model Residential Schools

  • Launched: 1998; revamped 2018.
  • Objective: Quality education for ST students.
  • Target: 740 EMRS by 2026.
  • Update 2024: 405 schools functional; 1 lakh students.

Example 16: What is the target number of EMRS?

740. Answer: 740.

10. Environment and Energy

10.1 National Solar Mission

  • Launched: 2010.
  • Objective: 100 GW solar by 2022; revised to 500 GW by 2030.
  • Achievements: 90 GW solar capacity (2024).
  • Update 2024: PM Suryaghar Yojana; rooftop solar for 100 million homes.

Example 17: What is India’s solar target by 2030?

500 GW. Answer: 500 GW.

10.2 Green India Mission

  • Launched: 2014.
  • Objective: Increase forest cover; carbon sink.
  • Target: 5 million hectares afforestation by 2030.
  • Update 2024: 2 million hectares covered; linked to net-zero 2070.

Example 18: What is the goal of Green India Mission?

Afforestation. Answer: Afforestation.

11. Key Policies (2024–2025)

  • Uniform Civil Code: Proposed; Uttarakhand implemented (2024).
  • National Health Policy 2017: Updated 2024; focus on NCDs.
  • One Nation One Election: Policy report submitted (2024).
  • Space Policy 2023: Enabled private launches; 100 startups (2024).

Example 19: Which state implemented UCC in 2024?

Uttarakhand. Answer: Uttarakhand.
