Geography Notes (General Awareness 2025)

1. Introduction to Geography

Geography studies the Earth’s physical features, human activities, and their interactions. For competitive exams (UPSC, SSC, CBSE Class 10), it covers physical (landforms, climate), human (population, urbanization), and economic geography (resources, industries), focusing on India and the world. It accounts for 8–12 questions in General Awareness, testing maps, features, and current affairs (2024–2025).

Importance: Geography shapes economies, cultures, and policies, critical for understanding global and national dynamics.

2. Physical Geography of India

2.1 Location and Size

  • Coordinates: 8°4'–37°6'N, 68°7'–97°25'E.
  • Area: 3.287 million km² (7th globally).
  • Coastline: 7,517 km; 9 coastal states.
  • Borders: Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, Myanmar, Afghanistan.

Example 1: What is India’s latitudinal extent?

8°4'–37°6'N. Answer: 8°4'–37°6'N.

Example 2: Which is India’s oldest landmass?

Peninsular Plateau. Answer: Peninsular Plateau.

3. Drainage System

  • Himalayan Rivers: Indus, Ganga, Brahmaputra; perennial.
  • Peninsular Rivers: Godavari, Krishna, Kaveri, Mahanadi; seasonal.
  • Projects: National River Linking Project, Ganga Action Plan.

Example 3: Which is India’s longest river?

Ganga (2,525 km). Answer: Ganga.

4. Climate of India

  • Type: Tropical monsoon; influenced by Himalayas, Thar Desert.
  • Seasons: Winter, Summer, Monsoon, Post-Monsoon.
  • Monsoon: Southwest (June–September), Northeast (October–December).
  • IMD: Indian Meteorological Department; forecasts cyclones, rains.

Example 4: What type of climate does India have?

Tropical monsoon. Answer: Tropical monsoon.

5. Natural Vegetation and Wildlife

  • Types: Tropical evergreen, deciduous, thorn, mangrove, alpine.
  • National Parks: Jim Corbett, Kaziranga, Sundarbans.
  • Conservation: Project Tiger (1973), Project Elephant (1992).
  • Biosphere Reserves: 18; Nilgiri, Sundarbans, Nanda Devi.

Example 5: Which project conserves tigers?

Project Tiger. Answer: Project Tiger.

7. Human Geography of India

7.1 Population

  • Total: 1.43 billion (2023, UN); surpassed China.
  • Density: 485/km² (2023); highest: Bihar.
  • Urbanization: 36% urban (2023); Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata.

7.2 Migration

  • Internal: Rural-urban (e.g., Bihar to Delhi).
  • International: Gulf countries, USA; remittances $100 billion (2024).

Example 7: Which state has highest population density?

Bihar. Answer: Bihar.

8. Economic Geography of India

8.1 Agriculture

  • Crops: Rice (UP), wheat (Punjab), tea (Assam), coffee (Karnataka).
  • Irrigation: 48% farmland irrigated; PMKSY scheme.
  • Revolutions: Green (foodgrains), White (milk), Blue (fish).

8.2 Minerals

  • Iron Ore: Odisha, Jharkhand; 5th globally.
  • Coal: Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh; 2nd globally.
  • Bauxite: Odisha, Gujarat.

8.3 Industries

  • Textiles: Mumbai, Ahmedabad.
  • IT: Bengaluru, Hyderabad.
  • Steel: Jamshedpur, Bhilai.

Example 8: Which state leads in iron ore production?

Odisha. Answer: Odisha.

9. Transport and Communication

  • Roads: 6.3 million km; NHs (2% length, 40% traffic).
  • Railways: 68,000 km; 4th largest network; Vande Bharat trains.
  • Ports: 13 major (Mumbai, Chennai, Kandla); Sagarmala project.
  • Digital: BharatNet, 5G rollout (2024).

Example 9: Which project boosts port development?

Sagarmala. Answer: Sagarmala.

10. World Physical Geography

  • Continents: 7 (Asia largest, Australia smallest).
  • Oceans: 5 (Pacific largest, Arctic smallest).
  • Mountains: Andes (longest), Himalayas (highest).
  • Rivers: Nile (longest, 6,650 km), Amazon (voluminous).

Example 10: Which is the longest river globally?

Nile. Answer: Nile.

11. World Human Geography

  • Population: 8.1 billion (2022); Asia 60%.
  • Urban Centers: Tokyo (largest, 37 million), Delhi, Shanghai.
  • Migration: Refugees (Ukraine, Syria); 281 million migrants (2024, UN).

Example 11: Which city has the largest population?

Tokyo. Answer: Tokyo.

12. World Economic Geography

  • Agriculture: Wheat (Russia), rice (China), coffee (Brazil).
  • Minerals: Oil (Saudi Arabia), gold (China), uranium (Kazakhstan).
  • Industries: Tech (USA), automobiles (Germany), textiles (China).

Example 12: Which country leads in oil production?

Saudi Arabia. Answer: Saudi Arabia.

13. Environmental Geography

  • Climate Change: Global warming; 1.1°C rise (2024, IPCC).
  • Disasters: Cyclones (Amphan 2020), floods (Kerala 2024).
  • Conservation: Paris Agreement (2015); India’s 2070 net-zero goal.

Example 13: What is India’s net-zero target year?

2070. Answer: 2070.

14. Recent Geographical Developments (2024–2025)

  • Disasters: Kerala floods (2024); Cyclone Chido (2024, Odisha).
  • Conservation: India adds 200th Ramsar site (2024, wetlands).
  • Infrastructure: Mumbai Coastal Road completed (2024).
  • Climate: India’s updated NDC; 50% renewable energy by 2030.

Example 14: Which state faced floods in 2024?

Kerala. Answer: Kerala.

15. Maps and Cartography

  • Types: Physical, political, thematic (e.g., rainfall).
  • Tools: GIS, remote sensing; ISRO’s Bhuvan portal.
  • Exam Tips: Mark rivers, mountains, states on blank maps.

Example 15: Which ISRO portal aids mapping?

Bhuvan. Answer: Bhuvan.
