Sports Notes (General Awareness 2025)

1. Introduction to Sports

Sports involve physical activities governed by rules, fostering fitness, teamwork, and competition. For competitive exams (UPSC, SSC, CBSE Class 10), Sports covers major games, tournaments, Indian and global achievements, and current affairs (e.g., Olympics, awards). Questions (5–10) appear in General Awareness sections, testing players, events, and records.

Importance: Sports promote health, national pride, and global unity. Knowledge of recent events (2024–2025) and historical milestones is crucial for exams.

2. History of Sports

Sports originated in ancient civilizations for training and entertainment.

  • Ancient India: Wrestling, archery (e.g., Mahabharata), polo.
  • Ancient Greece: Olympic Games (776 BCE), chariot racing.
  • Medieval Europe: Jousting, early football.
  • Modern Era: Codified rules (e.g., FIFA 1904, ICC 1909).

Example 1: Where did the Olympic Games originate?

Olympia, Greece (776 BCE). Answer: Greece.

3. Major Sports and Rules

3.1 Cricket

Popular in India, played by 11 players per team.

  • Formats: Test (5 days), ODI (50 overs), T20 (20 overs).
  • Rules: Batsmen score runs; bowlers aim for wickets; fielders prevent runs.
  • Governing Body: International Cricket Council (ICC).
  • Key Terms: LBW, DRS, powerplay.

Example 2: What is the duration of a T20 cricket match?

~3 hours (20 overs per team). Answer: ~3 hours.

3.2 Football

Global sport with 11 players per team.

  • Rules: Score goals by kicking ball into opponent’s net; 90 minutes (2 halves).
  • Governing Body: Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA).
  • Key Terms: Offside, penalty, VAR.

Example 3: How many players are on a football team?

11. Answer: 11.

3.3 Hockey

India’s national sport, played with sticks and a ball.

  • Rules: 11 players; score goals; 60 minutes (4 quarters).
  • Governing Body: International Hockey Federation (FIH).
  • Key Terms: Penalty corner, dribbling.

Example 4: What is India’s national sport?

Field hockey. Answer: Field hockey.

3.4 Badminton

Racquet sport, singles or doubles.

  • Rules: Hit shuttlecock over net; best of 3 games (21 points each).
  • Governing Body: Badminton World Federation (BWF).
  • Key Terms: Smash, rally, service.

3.5 Athletics

Includes running, jumping, throwing events.

  • Events: 100m sprint, long jump, javelin throw.
  • Governing Body: World Athletics.
  • Key Terms: False start, relay baton.

5. Indian Sports Achievements

India has excelled in cricket, hockey, badminton, and athletics.

  • Cricket: World Cups (1983, 2011); T20 WC (2007, 2024).
  • Hockey: 8 Olympic golds (1928–1980); 2021 Tokyo Olympics bronze.
  • Badminton: PV Sindhu (Olympic silver 2016, bronze 2020); Saina Nehwal (Olympic bronze 2012).
  • Athletics: Neeraj Chopra (Olympic javelin gold 2020).
  • Chess: Viswanathan Anand (5-time world champion); D Gukesh (2024 world champion).

Example 6: Who won India’s first Olympic javelin gold?

Neeraj Chopra (Tokyo 2020). Answer: Neeraj Chopra.

6. Recent Sports Events (2024–2025)

  • Paris Olympics 2024: India won 6 medals (1 silver, 5 bronze); Manu Bhaker (2 shooting bronzes), Neeraj Chopra (javelin silver).
  • T20 Cricket World Cup 2024: India defeated South Africa; Rohit Sharma captain.
  • Chess World Championship 2024: D Gukesh (India) defeated Ding Liren (China).
  • Asian Games 2023 (held 2024): India won 107 medals (29 gold), 4th rank.
  • ISL 2024–25: Mumbai City FC leads Indian Super League football.

Example 7: How many medals did India win at Paris Olympics 2024?

6 (1 silver, 5 bronze). Answer: 6.

8. Sports Awards in India

  • Dronacharya Award: For coaches (e.g., Pullela Gopichand, 2009).
  • Arjuna Award: For athletes (e.g., Harmanpreet Singh, 2024).
  • Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna: Highest sports honor; renamed Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna (e.g., Rohit Sharma, 2024).
  • Padma Awards: Padma Shri, Bhushan for sports (e.g., Mary Kom, Padma Bhushan 2020).

Example 9: What is India’s highest sports award?

Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna. Answer: Khel Ratna.

9. Sports Infrastructure in India

  • Stadia: Narendra Modi Stadium (Ahmedabad, largest globally, 132,000 capacity).
  • Schemes: Khelo India (2018) promotes grassroots sports; Fit India Movement.
  • Academies: SAI centers, BCCI’s National Cricket Academy.
  • Hosting: India to co-host Cricket World Cup 2023, 2036 Olympics bid.

Example 10: What is the world’s largest cricket stadium?

Narendra Modi Stadium. Answer: Narendra Modi Stadium.

10. Global Sports Trends

  • Esports: Gaming as sport (e.g., PUBG, FIFA eWorld Cup).
  • Women’s Sports: Rising viewership (e.g., WPL cricket).
  • Technology: VAR in football, Hawk-Eye in cricket.
  • Sustainability: Green Olympics (Paris 2024 used renewable energy).
  • Inclusion: Paralympics, Special Olympics growth.

Example 11: What technology reviews cricket decisions?

Decision Review System (DRS). Answer: DRS.

11. Olympic Games

Held every 4 years, summer and winter editions.

  • History: Revived in 1896 by Pierre de Coubertin.
  • India’s Record: 41 medals (10 gold, 9 silver, 22 bronze) till 2024; hockey dominates (8 golds).
  • Paris 2024: 329 events; India’s best non-hockey performance.
  • Los Angeles 2028: Next summer Olympics.

Example 12: Who revived the modern Olympics?

Pierre de Coubertin. Answer: Pierre de Coubertin.

12. Commonwealth Games

Held every 4 years for Commonwealth nations.

  • India’s Performance: 564 medals (181 gold) till 2022 Birmingham (26 medals, 7th rank).
  • Events: Athletics, wrestling, boxing (India’s strengths).
  • 2026: Glasgow, Scotland (slimmed-down events).

Example 13: Where were the 2022 Commonwealth Games held?

Birmingham, UK. Answer: Birmingham.

13. Asian Games

Multi-sport event for Asian nations.

  • History: Started 1951, New Delhi.
  • India’s Record: 885 medals (195 gold) till 2023 (held 2024, Hangzhou).
  • 2026: Nagoya, Japan.

Example 14: Where were the 2023 Asian Games held?

Hangzhou, China. Answer: Hangzhou.

14. Sports Schemes in India

  • Khelo India: Identifies talent, provides scholarships (₹5 lakh/year).
  • Target Olympic Podium Scheme (TOPS): Funds elite athletes (e.g., Neeraj Chopra).
  • Fit India: Promotes fitness through campaigns, school programs.
  • National Sports Policy 2024: Aims for 2036 Olympics hosting, sports economy.

Example 15: What scheme funds Olympic hopefuls?

Target Olympic Podium Scheme (TOPS). Answer: TOPS.

15. Sports and Society

Sports impact culture, economy, and health.

  • Economy: IPL (cricket) valued at $11 billion (2024).
  • Health: Reduces obesity, improves mental health.
  • Unity: Cricket unites India; Olympics foster global peace.
  • Challenges: Doping, match-fixing, gender pay gap.

Example 16: What is a major challenge in sports?

Doping (use of banned substances). Answer: Doping.

16. Women in Sports

Indian women have broken barriers in sports.

  • Mary Kom: Boxing, Olympic bronze (2012), 6 world titles.
  • PV Sindhu: Badminton, Olympic medals (2016, 2020).
  • Sakshi Malik: Wrestling, Olympic bronze (2016).
  • Mithali Raj: Cricket, most runs in women’s ODIs.
  • Initiatives: Women’s Premier League (WPL) cricket, Beti Bachao Beti Khelao.

Example 17: Who won India’s first women’s boxing Olympic medal?

Mary Kom (2012). Answer: Mary Kom.

17. Paralympics and Special Olympics

Inclusive sports for athletes with disabilities.

  • Paralympics 2024: India won 29 medals (7 gold), 9th rank; Avani Lekhara (2 shooting golds).
  • Special Olympics: For intellectual disabilities; India won 200+ medals in 2023.
  • Support: Paralympic Committee of India, TOPS funding.

Example 18: How many medals did India win at Paralympics 2024?

29 (7 gold). Answer: 29.
