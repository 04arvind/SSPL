Personalities Notes (General Awareness 2025)

1. Introduction to Personalities

Personalities refer to individuals who have significantly influenced society through their contributions in politics, science, sports, arts, literature, social reform, and other fields. For competitive exams (UPSC, SSC, CBSE Class 10), this topic covers Indian and global figures, their achievements, awards, and recent developments (2024–2025). It accounts for 5–8 questions in General Awareness, testing biographical details, contributions, and current affairs.

Importance: Studying personalities highlights leadership, innovation, and societal change, crucial for understanding history and current events.

2. Indian Historical Personalities

2.1 Freedom Fighters

  • Mahatma Gandhi: Led non-violent independence movement; Salt March (1930); assassinated 1948.
  • Bhagat Singh: Revolutionary; bombed Central Legislative Assembly (1929); executed 1931.
  • Subhas Chandra Bose: Founded INA; “Give me blood, I’ll give you freedom”; died 1945.
  • Rani Lakshmibai: Led 1857 Revolt in Jhansi; died in battle 1858.
  • Sardar Patel: Unified 562 princely states; first Home Minister; died 1950.

Example 1: Who led the Dandi Salt March?

Mahatma Gandhi (1930). Answer: Mahatma Gandhi.

2.2 Social Reformers

  • Raja Ram Mohan Roy: Founded Brahmo Samaj (1828); abolished Sati (1829); died 1833.
  • Jyotiba Phule: Promoted women’s education; founded Satyashodhak Samaj (1873); died 1890.
  • BR Ambedkar: Drafted Indian Constitution; fought untouchability; died 1956.
  • Savitribai Phule: First female teacher; opened girls’ school (1848); died 1897.

Example 2: Who abolished Sati in 1829?

Raja Ram Mohan Roy. Answer: Raja Ram Mohan Roy.

3. Indian Political Personalities

  • Jawaharlal Nehru: First PM (1947–1964); authored Discovery of India; died 1964.
  • Indira Gandhi: First female PM (1966–1977, 1980–1984); Operation Blue Star; assassinated 1984.
  • Atal Bihari Vajpayee: PM (1998–2004); Pokhran-II tests (1998); died 2018.
  • Narendra Modi: PM since 2014; launched Digital India, Swachh Bharat; active 2025.
  • APJ Abdul Kalam: President (2002–2007); missile scientist; died 2015.

Example 3: Who was India’s first female Prime Minister?

Indira Gandhi. Answer: Indira Gandhi.

4. Indian Scientists

  • CV Raman: Nobel Prize in Physics (1930) for Raman Effect; died 1970.
  • Homi J Bhabha: Founded TIFR, BARC; nuclear program pioneer; died 1966.
  • Vikram Sarabhai: Father of ISRO; founded PRL; died 1971.
  • Satyendra Nath Bose: Bose-Einstein statistics; died 1974.
  • MS Swaminathan: Green Revolution leader; died 2023.

Example 4: Who won India’s first Nobel Prize in Physics?

CV Raman (1930). Answer: CV Raman.

5. Indian Sports Personalities

  • Sachin Tendulkar: Cricket; 100 international centuries; retired 2013.
  • Dhyan Chand: Hockey; 3 Olympic golds (1928–1936); died 1979.
  • PV Sindhu: Badminton; Olympic silver (2016), bronze (2020); active 2025.
  • Neeraj Chopra: Javelin; Olympic gold (2020), silver (2024); active 2025.
  • D Gukesh: Chess; world champion 2024; active 2025.

Example 5: Who won the 2024 Chess World Championship?

D Gukesh. Answer: D Gukesh.

6. Indian Literary Figures

  • Rabindranath Tagore: Nobel Prize in Literature (1913) for Gitanjali; died 1941.
  • Munshi Premchand: Hindi-Urdu novelist; Godan; died 1936.
  • Amrita Pritam: Punjabi poet; Ajj Aakhaan Waris Shah Nu; died 2005.
  • RK Narayan: English novelist; Malgudi Days; died 2001.
  • Arundhati Roy: The God of Small Things (Booker 1997); active 2025.

Example 6: Who won the Nobel Prize for Gitanjali?

Rabindranath Tagore (1913). Answer: Rabindranath Tagore.

7. Indian Artists and Musicians

  • MS Subbulakshmi: Carnatic vocalist; Bharat Ratna (1998); died 2004.
  • MF Husain: Modern painter; Horses series; died 2011.
  • Abanindranath Tagore: Bengal School founder; died 1951.

Lata Mangeshkar. Answer: Lata Mangeshkar.

8. Global Historical Personalities

  • Albert Einstein: Physicist; E=mc²; Nobel Prize (1921); died 1955.

  • Abraham Lincoln: US President; abolished slavery; assassinated 1865.
  • Marie Curie: Scientist; 2 Nobel Prizes (1903, 1911); died 1934.
  • Martin Luther King Jr: Civil rights leader; “I Have a Dream”; assassinated 1968.

Albert Einstein. Answer: Albert Einstein.

24. Study Tips

  • Group by Field: Memorize personalities by category (e.g., sports, science).
  • Current Affairs: Follow The Hindu, PIB for recent news (e.g., awards).
  • Mnemonics: “TAGRAM” for Nobel winners (Tagore, Ambedkar, etc.).
  • Flashcards: Name, field, key achievement.
  • Mock Tests: Solve UPSC/SSC personality MCQs.

25. Revision Strategy

  • Daily: Read one personality profile (e.g., news, biography).
  • Weekly: Revise one category (e.g., scientists).
  • Monthly: Solve 50 personality MCQs.
  • Timelines: Chart key figures by era.
  • Group Study: Discuss recent personalities in news.
