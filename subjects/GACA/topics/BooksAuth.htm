Books and Authors Notes (General Awareness 2025)

1. Introduction to Books and Authors

Books and Authors is a key topic in General Awareness, covering significant literary works and their creators, both Indian and global. For competitive exams (UPSC, SSC, CBSE Class 10), it includes recent publications (2024–2025), award-winning books, and classics, accounting for 5–8 questions. Focus areas are fiction, non-fiction, biographies, and socio-political works.

Importance: Books reflect cultural, historical, and political trends, essential for understanding contemporary issues.

2. Indian Non-Fiction (2014–2025)

2.1 Political and Historical Works

  • Book: The Idea of India (2024) by Shashi Tharoor.
    • Theme: India’s democratic journey post-1947.
    • Relevance: UPSC, SSC; nationalism debates.
  • Book: Why Bharat Matters (2024) by S. Jaishankar.
    • Theme: India’s foreign policy in a multipolar world.
    • Relevance: International relations, current affairs.
  • Book: The New India (2023) by Arun Jaitley (posthumous).
    • Theme: Economic reforms, GST impact.
    • Relevance: Economic policy questions.

Example 1: Who wrote Why Bharat Matters?

S. Jaishankar. Answer: S. Jaishankar.

2.2 Biographies and Memoirs

  • Book: Naam, Namak, Nishan (2024) by General Bipin Rawat (posthumous).
    • Theme: Life of India’s first CDS.
    • Relevance: Defence, leadership.
  • Book: My Life in Full (2021) by Indra Nooyi.
    • Theme: Journey of PepsiCo’s ex-CEO.
    • Relevance: Women empowerment, corporate governance.
  • Book: Unfinished (2021) by Priyanka Chopra Jonas.

General Bipin Rawat. Answer: General Bipin Rawat.

19. Study Tips

  • Lists: Memorize books by awards, authors.
  • Current Affairs: Follow literary news (PIB, The Hindu).
  • Mnemonics: “PNB” for Political, Non-fiction, Biographies.
  • Tables: Group books by genre, year.
  • Mock Tests: Solve UPSC/SSC literature MCQs.

20. Revision Strategy

  • Daily: Learn 2–3 books/authors.
  • Weekly: Revise one category (e.g., fiction).
  • Monthly: Solve 50 literature MCQs.
  • Flashcards: Books, authors, awards.
  • Group Study: Discuss 2024 books (e.g., Why Bharat Matters).
