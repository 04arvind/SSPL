Politics Notes (General Awareness 2025)

1. Introduction to Politics

Politics involves the governance of societies through decision-making, institutions, and policies. For competitive exams (UPSC, SSC, CBSE Class 10), Politics covers Indian polity (Constitution, institutions), global political systems, and current affairs (2024–2025). It accounts for 10–15 questions in General Awareness, testing structures, leaders, and events.

Importance: Politics shapes governance, rights, and international relations, critical for understanding national and global dynamics.

2. Political Systems

2.1 Types of Government

  • Democracy: Rule by people; direct (referendum) or representative (India, USA).
  • Monarchy: Rule by king/queen; absolute (Saudi Arabia) or constitutional (UK).
  • Dictatorship: Single ruler; authoritarian (North Korea).
  • Theocracy: Religious rule (Iran).
  • Republic: Elected head of state (India).

Example 1: What type of government does India have?

Democratic Republic. Answer: Democratic Republic.

2.2 Political Ideologies

  • Capitalism: Private ownership, free markets (USA).
  • Socialism: State-controlled resources (Cuba).
  • Communism: Classless society, state ownership (China).
  • Secularism: State neutrality on religion (India).

Example 2: What ideology emphasizes state neutrality on religion?

Secularism. Answer: Secularism.

3. Indian Political System

3.1 Constitution of India

  • Adopted: November 26, 1949; effective January 26, 1950.
  • Features: Federal, parliamentary, secular, fundamental rights/duties.
  • Preamble: Sovereign, socialist, secular, democratic republic.
  • Amendments: 106 (2024); 42nd (1976) most significant.

Example 3: When was the Indian Constitution adopted?

November 26, 1949. Answer: November 26, 1949.

3.2 Structure of Government

  • Union: President, Parliament (Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha), PM, Council of Ministers.
  • State: Governor, State Legislature, CM, Council of Ministers.
  • Judiciary: Supreme Court, High Courts, District Courts.

Example 4: What is India’s lower house of Parliament?

Lok Sabha. Answer: Lok Sabha.

5. Political Parties in India

  • National Parties: BJP, Congress, CPI(M), AAP, BSP, NPP, CPI.
  • Regional Parties: DMK (Tamil Nadu), TMC (West Bengal), SP (UP).
  • Alliances: NDA (BJP-led), INDIA (Congress-led).

Example 6: Which party leads the NDA coalition?

BJP. Answer: BJP.

6. Elections in India

  • Lok Sabha: 543 seats; every 5 years; last: 2024 (18th Lok Sabha).
  • Rajya Sabha: 245 seats; 1/3 retire every 2 years; indirect election.
  • State Assemblies: Vary by state (e.g., UP: 403 seats).
  • 2024 Elections: BJP won 240 seats; NDA 293; Modi’s 3rd term.

Example 7: How many seats did BJP win in 2024 Lok Sabha?

240. Answer: 240.

7. Federalism in India

  • Division of Powers: Union List (97), State List (66), Concurrent List (47).
  • Finance: Finance Commission (15th, 2021–2026); GST Council.
  • Issues: Centre-state tensions (e.g., fund devolution).

Example 8: Which list includes defence and foreign affairs?

Union List. Answer: Union List.

9. International Organizations

  • UN: Founded 1945; 193 members; HQ: New York; Secretary-General: António Guterres.
  • G20: 19 countries + EU; India hosted 2023; 2025 host: South Africa.
  • BRICS: Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa; expanded 2024 (Iran, UAE).
  • SAARC: 8 South Asian nations; HQ: Kathmandu.
  • ASEAN: 10 Southeast Asian nations; India dialogue partner.

Example 10: Who is the UN Secretary-General in 2025?

António Guterres. Answer: António Guterres.

10. Indian Foreign Policy

  • Principles: Non-Alignment, Panchsheel, strategic autonomy.
  • Initiatives: Act East Policy, Neighbourhood First, Vaccine Maitri.
  • Key Partners: USA (QUAD), Russia (defence), Japan (infrastructure).
  • Challenges: China border tensions, Pakistan (terrorism).

Example 11: What is India’s policy for ASEAN engagement?

Act East Policy. Answer: Act East Policy.

  • Lok Sabha Elections 2024: NDA won; Modi sworn in June 2024.
  • State Elections 2024: Maharashtra (BJP-led win), Jharkhand (JMM-led).
  • Legislation: Waqf Amendment Bill 2024; UCC talks ongoing.
  • Judiciary: Sanjiv Khanna appointed CJI (November 2024).
  • Foreign Policy: Modi’s Ukraine visit (2024) for peace talks.

13. Political Reforms in India

  • Electoral: One Nation One Election proposed (2024).
  • Administrative: Mission Karmayogi (civil service training).
  • Judicial: National Judicial Appointments Commission (struck down 2015).
  • Local Governance: 73rd/74th Amendments (1992) for PRIs, ULBs.

Example 14: What empowers Panchayati Raj?

73rd Amendment. Answer: 73rd Amendment.

14. Women in Indian Politics

  • Leaders: Indira Gandhi (ex-PM), Droupadi Murmu (President), Mamata Banerjee (CM).
  • Reservation: Women’s Reservation Bill 2023 (33% in legislatures).
  • Participation: 74 women MPs in 18th Lok Sabha (2024).

Example 15: What reserves 33% seats for women in legislatures?

Women’s Reservation Bill 2023. Answer: Women’s Reservation Bill.
