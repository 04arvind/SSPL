Indian Polity Notes (General Awareness 2025)

1. Introduction to Indian Polity

Indian Polity covers the structure, functions, and principles of India’s governance, rooted in its Constitution. For competitive exams (UPSC, SSC, CBSE Class 10), it includes the Constitution, institutions (executive, legislature, judiciary), federalism, and current affairs (2024–2025). It accounts for 12–18 questions in General Awareness, testing articles, amendments, and recent political developments.

Importance: Polity shapes India’s democratic framework, rights, and governance, critical for understanding national policies.

2. Indian Constitution

2.1 Overview

  • Adopted: November 26, 1949; effective January 26, 1950.
  • Length: Longest written Constitution; 470 articles, 12 schedules.
  • Preamble: Sovereign, socialist, secular, democratic republic; justice, liberty, equality, fraternity.
  • Sources: UK (Parliament), USA (fundamental rights), Ireland (DPSP).

Example 1: When was the Indian Constitution effective?

January 26, 1950. Answer: January 26, 1950.

2.2 Key Features

  • Federalism: Union and states; quasi-federal structure.
  • Parliamentary System: PM as head of government.
  • Fundamental Rights: 6 (Articles 12–35).
  • DPSPs: Directive Principles (Articles 36–51).
  • Fundamental Duties: 11 (Article 51A).

Example 2: How many fundamental rights are there?

Six. Answer: Six.

4. Union Executive

  • President: Head of state; elected by electoral college; 5-year term; Droupadi Murmu (2022–).
  • Vice-President: Ex-officio Rajya Sabha Chairman; Jagdeep Dhankhar (2022–).
  • Prime Minister: Head of government; Narendra Modi (2014–).
  • Council of Ministers: Advises President; Cabinet led by PM.

Example 4: Who is India’s President in 2025?

Droupadi Murmu. Answer: Droupadi Murmu.

5. Parliament of India

  • Bicameral: Lok Sabha (lower, 543 seats), Rajya Sabha (upper, 245 seats).
  • Lok Sabha: Directly elected; 5-year term; Speaker: Om Birla (2024–).
  • Rajya Sabha: Indirectly elected; permanent body; Chairman: VP.
  • Functions: Legislation, budget approval, oversight.

Example 5: Who is the Lok Sabha Speaker in 2025?

Om Birla. Answer: Om Birla.

6. Judiciary

  • Supreme Court: Apex court; CJI Sanjiv Khanna (2024–).
  • High Courts: 25; state-level; appoint judges.
  • Independence: Collegium system, fixed tenure.
  • Functions: Constitutional interpretation, PILs, appeals.

Example 6: Who is the Chief Justice of India in 2025?

Sanjiv Khanna. Answer: Sanjiv Khanna.

7. State Government

  • Governor: State head; appointed by President.
  • Chief Minister: Head of state government; elected by legislature.
  • State Legislature: Unicameral (e.g., UP) or bicameral (e.g., Karnataka).
  • Functions: State laws, budget, administration.

Example 7: Who appoints state Governors?

President. Answer: President.

8. Federalism

  • Division of Powers: Union List (97), State List (66), Concurrent List (47).
  • Finance Commission: 15th (2021–2026); devolves funds.
  • GST Council: Unified tax; chaired by Finance Minister.
  • Issues: Centre-state disputes (e.g., fund allocation).

Example 8: Which list covers education?

Concurrent List. Answer: Concurrent List.

9. Local Governance

  • Panchayati Raj: 73rd Amendment (1992); 3-tier system (village, block, district).
  • Urban Bodies: 74th Amendment; municipalities, corporations.
  • Reservation: 33% for women in PRIs, ULBs.

Example 9: Which amendment empowered Panchayati Raj?

73rd Amendment. Answer: 73rd Amendment.

10. Constitutional Amendments

  • Total: 106 (2024).
  • Key Amendments:
    • 42nd (1976): Added socialist, secular to Preamble.
    • 44th (1978): Restored judicial review.
    • 73rd/74th (1992): Local governance.
    • 101st (2016): Introduced GST.

Example 10: Which amendment introduced GST?

101st Amendment. Answer: 101st Amendment.

11. Election System

  • Election Commission: Independent body; Chief: Rajiv Kumar (2025).
  • Lok Sabha: 543 seats; FPTP system; 2024 elections (18th Lok Sabha).
  • Rajya Sabha: 245 seats; proportional representation.
  • Reforms: One Nation One Election proposed (2024).

Example 11: Who heads the Election Commission in 2025?

Rajiv Kumar. Answer: Rajiv Kumar.

12. Political Parties and Coalitions

  • National Parties: BJP, Congress, CPI(M), AAP, BSP, NPP, CPI.
  • Regional Parties: TMC (West Bengal), DMK (Tamil Nadu), SP (UP).
  • Coalitions: NDA (BJP-led), INDIA (Congress-led).

Example 12: Which coalition is led by BJP?

NDA. Answer: NDA.

13. Emergency Provisions

  • National Emergency: Article 352; last imposed 1975–1977.
  • President’s Rule: Article 356; state government dismissal.
  • Financial Emergency: Article 360; never imposed.

Example 13: Which article deals with National Emergency?

Article 352. Answer: Article 352.

  • Legislation: Waqf Amendment Bill 2024; UCC discussions ongoing.
  • Judiciary: CJI Sanjiv Khanna appointed (November 2024).
  • Reforms: One Nation One Election committee report (2024).
  • Women’s Reservation: 33% seats reserved (2023, implementation pending).

17. Women in Indian Polity

  • Leaders: Droupadi Murmu (President), Indira Gandhi (ex-PM), Nirmala Sitharaman (FM).
  • Participation: 74 women MPs in 18th Lok Sabha (2024).
  • Reservation: 33% in PRIs, ULBs; 2023 Bill for legislatures.

18. Challenges in Indian Polity

  • Corruption: Lokpal Act 2013; limited impact.
  • Centre-State Tensions: GST, fund devolution disputes.
  • Judicial Delays: 5 crore pending cases (2024).
  • Electoral Issues: Money power, voter apathy.

Example 18: What is a challenge in Indian polity?

Judicial delays. Answer: Judicial delays.
