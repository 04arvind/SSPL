Culture Notes (General Awareness 2025)

1. Introduction to Culture

Culture encompasses traditions, beliefs, art, language, festivals, and lifestyles of a society. For competitive exams (UPSC, SSC, CBSE Class 10), Culture covers Indian heritage (art, dance, music, literature), global cultural influences, and current affairs (e.g., UNESCO sites, festivals). It accounts for 5–10 questions in General Awareness, testing historical and contemporary cultural knowledge.

Importance: Culture reflects identity, promotes unity in diversity, and preserves heritage. Exam questions focus on classical arts, festivals, and recent cultural developments (2024–2025).

2. Indian Culture: Overview

India’s culture is one of the oldest, dating back to the Indus Valley Civilization (3300–1300 BCE). It blends Vedic traditions, regional diversity, and modern influences.

  • Unity in Diversity: 28 states, 8 UTs, 22 official languages, multiple religions (Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, Sikhism, etc.).
  • Heritage: Temples, forts, classical arts, literature (Vedas, epics).
  • Global Impact: Yoga, Ayurveda, Bollywood, Indian diaspora.

Example 1: What ancient civilization shaped early Indian culture?

Indus Valley Civilization. Answer: Indus Valley.

4. Folk Dances

Folk dances reflect regional traditions, performed during festivals and celebrations.

  • Garba/Dandiya: Gujarat, Navratri, circular dance with sticks.
  • Bhangra/Gidda: Punjab, harvest festival, energetic.
  • Lavani: Maharashtra, rhythmic, performed by women.
  • Chhau: Odisha/Jharkhand/West Bengal, martial arts-based, masked.
  • Bihu: Assam, harvest festival, vibrant.

Example 3: Which folk dance is associated with Navratri?

Garba/Dandiya. Answer: Garba.

5. Classical Music

Indian classical music has two traditions: Hindustani (North India) and Carnatic (South India).

  • Hindustani: Improvisation, ragas (e.g., Bhairav), artists like Ravi Shankar.
  • Carnatic: Structured, kritis, ragas (e.g., Hamsadhwani), artists like MS Subbulakshmi.
  • Instruments: Sitar, tabla (Hindustani); veena, mridangam (Carnatic).
  • Gharanas: Schools like Gwalior, Jaipur (Hindustani).

Example 4: Who is a renowned Carnatic vocalist?

MS Subbulakshmi. Answer: MS Subbulakshmi.

6. Indian Literature

Spans ancient texts to modern works in multiple languages.

  • Ancient: Vedas, Upanishads, Ramayana, Mahabharata.
  • Medieval: Bhakti poetry (Tulsidas, Kabir), Sufi works (Amir Khusro).
  • Modern: Tagore (Gitanjali, Nobel 1913), Premchand (Hindi), Amrita Pritam (Punjabi).
  • Languages: Sanskrit, Tamil, Bengali, Hindi, Malayalam, etc.

Example 5: Who wrote the Ramayana?

Valmiki. Answer: Valmiki.

8. Indian Art and Architecture

Spans ancient to modern, reflecting diverse influences.

  • Ancient: Indus seals, Mauryan pillars, Ajanta-Ellora caves (Buddhist).
  • Classical: Khajuraho temples (Hindu), Sanchi Stupa (Buddhist).
  • Medieval: Mughal (Taj Mahal), Dravidian temples (Madurai Meenakshi).
  • Modern: Bengal School (Abanindranath Tagore), MF Husain paintings.

Example 7: Which monument was built by Shah Jahan?

Taj Mahal. Answer: Taj Mahal.

9. UNESCO World Heritage Sites in India

India has 43 UNESCO sites (35 cultural, 7 natural, 1 mixed) as of 2025.

  • Cultural: Taj Mahal (1983), Red Fort (2007), Chola Temples (1987).
  • Natural: Western Ghats (2012), Sundarbans (1987).
  • Mixed: Khangchendzonga National Park (2016).
  • Recent: Hoysala Temples (2024, Karnataka), Moidams of Assam (2024).

Example 8: Which Indian site was added to UNESCO in 2024?

Hoysala Temples. Answer: Hoysala Temples.

10. Indian Cinema

Bollywood and regional cinema shape global perceptions.

  • Bollywood: Hindi cinema, Mumbai; global reach (e.g., RRR, 2022 Oscar win).
  • Regional: Tamil (Rajinikanth), Telugu (Baahubali), Malayalam (award-winning).
  • Pioneers: Dadasaheb Phalke (first film, Raja Harishchandra, 1913).
  • Festivals: IFFI (Goa), Cannes (India pavilion 2024).

Example 9: Who is the father of Indian cinema?

Dadasaheb Phalke. Answer: Dadasaheb Phalke.

11. Indian Handicrafts and Textiles

Reflect regional skills and traditions.

  • Handicrafts: Madhubani painting (Bihar), Pashmina shawls (Kashmir).
  • Textiles: Banarasi sarees (UP), Kanjeevaram silk (Tamil Nadu).
  • GI Tags: Darjeeling tea, Kutch embroidery (2024: 1,200+ GI tags).

Example 10: Which saree is famous from Varanasi?

Banarasi saree. Answer: Banarasi saree.

12. Indian Cuisine

Diverse, reflecting geography and culture.

  • North: Butter chicken, naan, tandoori.
  • South: Dosa, idli, sambhar.
  • East: Rasgulla, fish curry.
  • West: Vada pav, dhokla.
  • Global Impact: Indian spices, curry worldwide.

Example 11: Which sweet is famous in West Bengal?

Rasgulla. Answer: Rasgulla.

13. Yoga and Ayurveda

Ancient Indian systems with global influence.

  • Yoga: Physical, mental discipline; International Yoga Day (June 21, UN 2014).
  • Ayurveda: Herbal medicine, holistic healing (e.g., turmeric, ashwagandha).
  • Institutions: AYUSH Ministry, Patanjali Research Institute.

Example 12: When is International Yoga Day celebrated?

June 21. Answer: June 21.

14. Cultural Institutions in India

  • Sangeet Natak Akademi: Promotes music, dance, drama.
  • Sahitya Akademi: Literature in 24 languages.
  • Archaeological Survey of India (ASI): Protects 3,600+ monuments.
  • National School of Drama: Theatre training, Delhi.
  • IGNCA: Indira Gandhi National Centre for Arts.

Example 13: Which body protects India’s monuments?

Archaeological Survey of India. Answer: ASI.

15. Global Cultural Influences

Indian culture influences and is influenced globally.

  • Diaspora: 32 million NRIs spread Indian festivals, cuisine.
  • Bollywood: Global fanbase in Middle East, Africa.
  • Western Impact: Pop music, fast food in urban India.
  • Soft Power: India’s G20 presidency 2023 promoted culture.

Example 14: What Indian film won an Oscar in 2023?

RRR (Naatu Naatu, Best Original Song). Answer: RRR.

16. Recent Cultural Developments (2024–2025)

  • UNESCO Listings 2024: Hoysala Temples, Moidams of Assam added.
  • Kumbh Mela 2025: Prayagraj, world’s largest religious gathering.
  • GI Tags 2024: Odisha’s Konark stone carving, Kashmir’s saffron.
  • IFFI 2024: 55th International Film Festival of India, Goa.
  • Sahitya Akademi Awards 2024: Hindi: Mrityunjay for Shankhnaad.

Example 15: Where will Kumbh Mela 2025 be held?

Prayagraj. Answer: Prayagraj.

17. Cultural Preservation

Efforts to protect India’s heritage.

  • Schemes: HRIDAY, PRASHAD for heritage cities, pilgrimage sites.
  • Digital: National Digital Library, ASI’s 3D monument scans.
  • Challenges: Urbanization, climate change, funding.
  • NGOs: INTACH, SPIC MACAY promote arts, heritage.

Example 16: What scheme restores heritage cities?

HRIDAY. Answer: HRIDAY.

18. Tribal Culture

India’s 700+ tribes contribute unique traditions.

  • Examples: Gond (Madhya Pradesh), Santhal (Jharkhand), Naga (Nagaland).
  • Art: Warli painting (Maharashtra), Pattachitra (Odisha).
  • Festivals: Hornbill (Nagaland), Bhagoria (MP).
  • Protection: Tribal Sub-Plan, Forest Rights Act 2006.

Example 17: Which tribe is known for Warli painting?

Warli (Maharashtra). Answer: Warli.

19. Cultural Diplomacy

India promotes culture globally.

  • ICC: Indian Cultural Centres in 40 countries.
  • G20 2023: Showcased Bharatanatyam, handicrafts.
  • Namaste Bharat: Cultural festivals abroad (2024: USA, UK).
  • UNESCO: India’s role in ICH (Intangible Cultural Heritage) list.

Example 18: What dance was showcased at G20 2023?

Bharatanatyam. Answer: Bharatanatyam.
