Current Events Notes (General Awareness 2025)

1. Introduction to Current Events

Current Events cover significant national and international developments in politics, economy, environment, science, and society for 2024–2025. For competitive exams (UPSC, SSC, CBSE Class 10), it accounts for 10–15 questions in General Awareness, testing recent policies, summits, elections, and disasters. Staying updated via newspapers (The Hindu, PIB) and reliable sources is crucial.

Importance: Current Events reflect dynamic global and national trends, essential for understanding contemporary issues.

2. Indian Political Events (2024–2025)

2.1 Lok Sabha Elections 2024

  • Details: 18th Lok Sabha; April–June 2024; 543 seats.
  • Results: NDA (BJP-led) won 293 seats; BJP 240; INDIA (Congress-led) 234.
  • Impact: Narendra Modi sworn in for 3rd term (June 9, 2024).

Example 1: Who won the 2024 Lok Sabha elections?

NDA. Answer: NDA.

2.2 State Elections

  • Maharashtra: November 2024; BJP-led Mahayuti won 235/288 seats.
  • Jharkhand: November 2024; JMM-led alliance retained power.
  • Delhi: Scheduled February 2025; AAP vs. BJP key contest.

Example 2: Which alliance won Maharashtra in 2024?

Mahayuti. Answer: Mahayuti.

2.3 Legislative Developments

  • Waqf Amendment Bill 2024: Reforms Waqf property management.
  • UCC: Uniform Civil Code discussions ongoing; Uttarakhand implemented (2024).
  • One Nation One Election: Committee report submitted (2024).

Example 3: What bill reformed Waqf in 2024?

Waqf Amendment Bill. Answer: Waqf Amendment Bill.

3. Indian Economic Events

  • GDP Growth: 6.7% (2024–25, projected); 5th largest economy ($3.9 trillion).
  • Budget 2024–25: Rs. 48.21 lakh crore; focus on infra, green energy.
  • Digital Economy: UPI transactions hit 16 billion/month (2024).
  • Inflation: CPI at 5.5% (2024); RBI targets 4%.

Example 4: What is India’s projected GDP growth for 2024–25?

6.7%. Answer: 6.7%.

4. Environmental Events

  • Kerala Floods: July 2024; Wayanad landslides; 300+ deaths.
  • Cyclone Chido: December 2024; hit Odisha, Tamil Nadu.
  • Conservation: India’s 200th Ramsar site added (2024, wetlands).
  • Renewable Energy: 50% non-fossil fuel target by 2030; 90 GW solar (2024).

5. Science and Technology Events
