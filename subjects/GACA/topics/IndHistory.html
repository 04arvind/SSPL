Indian History Notes (General Awareness 2025)

1. Introduction to Indian History

Indian history spans thousands of years, from ancient civilizations to modern independence. For competitive exams (UPSC, SSC, CBSE Class 10), it covers ancient, medieval, modern periods, key events, personalities, and recent historical developments (2024–2025). It accounts for 10–15 questions in General Awareness, testing timelines, movements, and archaeological findings.

Importance: Indian history reflects cultural, political, and social evolution, shaping national identity and global contributions.

2. Ancient Indian History

2.1 Indus Valley Civilization (3300–1300 BCE)

  • Sites: Harappa, Mohenjo-daro, Dholavira, Lothal.
  • Features: Urban planning, drainage, seals, script (undeciphered).
  • Economy: Agriculture, trade (Mesopotamia).
  • Decline: Climate change, Aryan migration (debated).

Example 1: Which Indus Valley site is in Gujarat?

Dholavira. Answer: Dholavira.

2.2 Vedic Period (1500–600 BCE)

  • Texts: Rigveda, Samaveda, Yajurveda, Atharvaveda.
  • Society: Tribal, pastoral; varna system emerged.
  • Economy: Agriculture, cattle-based.

Example 2: Which is the oldest Veda?

Rigveda. Answer: Rigveda.

2.3 Mahajanapadas and Mauryan Empire

  • Mahajanapadas: 16 kingdoms (600–300 BCE); Magadha dominant.
  • Mauryan Empire: Chandragupta Maurya (321–297 BCE); Ashoka (268–232 BCE); Kalinga War, Buddhism spread.
  • Administration: Arthashastra (Kautilya), centralized rule.

Example 3: Who wrote Arthashastra?

Kautilya. Answer: Kautilya.

3. Post-Mauryan and Gupta Period

3.1 Post-Mauryan

  • Kingdoms: Shungas, Kushanas, Satavahanas.
  • Trade: Silk Road, Roman contacts.
  • Art: Sanchi Stupa, Bharhut.

3.2 Gupta Empire (320–550 CE)

  • Rulers: Chandragupta I, Samudragupta, Chandragupta II.
  • Golden Age: Science (Aryabhata), literature (Kalidasa), art (Ajanta).
  • Decline: Huna invasions.

Example 4: Who wrote Meghaduta?

Kalidasa. Answer: Kalidasa.

4. Medieval Indian History

4.1 Early Medieval (600–1200 CE)

  • Kingdoms: Cholas, Chalukyas, Pallavas, Rajputs.
  • Chola Empire: Rajaraja I, Rajendra I; naval power, Brihadeshwara Temple.
  • Culture: Bhakti (Alvars, Nayanars), temple architecture.

Example 5: Which Chola king built Brihadeshwara Temple?

Rajaraja I. Answer: Rajaraja I.

4.2 Delhi Sultanate (1206–1526)

  • Dynasties: Mamluk, Khilji, Tughlaq, Sayyid, Lodi.
  • Rulers: Alauddin Khilji (market reforms), Muhammad Tughlaq (token currency).
  • Culture: Indo-Islamic architecture (Qutub Minar).

Example 6: Who built Qutub Minar?

Qutbuddin Aibak. Answer: Qutbuddin Aibak.

4.3 Mughal Empire (1526–1857)

  • Rulers: Babur (Panipat 1526), Akbar (Din-i-Ilahi), Shah Jahan (Taj Mahal), Aurangzeb (orthodox).
  • Administration: Mansabdari, zamindari.
  • Decline: Maratha rise, British intervention.

Example 7: Who founded the Mughal Empire?

Babur (1526). Answer: Babur.

5. Modern Indian History

5.1 Colonial Rule (1757–1857)

  • East India Company: Battle of Plassey (1757); subsidiary alliance, doctrine of lapse.
  • Revolt of 1857: First War of Independence; Mangal Pandey, Rani Lakshmibai; ended Company rule.

Example 8: Who led Jhansi in 1857 Revolt?

Rani Lakshmibai. Answer: Rani Lakshmibai.

5.2 Freedom Struggle (1857–1947)

  • INC: Founded 1885; moderates (Gokhale), extremists (Tilak).
  • Gandhian Era: Non-Cooperation (1920), Civil Disobedience (1930), Quit India (1942).
  • Revolutionaries: Bhagat Singh, Subhas Bose (INA).
  • Independence: August 15, 1947; Partition (India, Pakistan).

Example 9: When was the Quit India Movement launched?

1942. Answer: 1942.

6. Post-Independence India

  • Constitution: Adopted 1949; effective 1950; Ambedkar’s role.
  • Integration: Sardar Patel unified 562 princely states.
  • Nehru Era: Mixed economy, Non-Alignment, Five-Year Plans.
  • Wars: Indo-China (1962), Indo-Pak (1947, 1965, 1971).

Example 10: Who drafted India’s Constitution?

BR Ambedkar. Answer: BR Ambedkar.

7. Social and Cultural Movements

  • Bhakti-Sufi: Kabir, Nanak, Amir Khusro; promoted unity.
  • Reforms: Raja Ram Mohan Roy (Sati ban), Jyotiba Phule (education).
  • Tribal: Birsa Munda (Ulgulan), Santhal Rebellion (1855).

Example 11: Who abolished Sati in 1829?

Raja Ram Mohan Roy. Answer: Raja Ram Mohan Roy.

8. Economic History

  • Ancient: Indus trade, Mauryan taxation.
  • Medieval: Mughal land revenue (Todar Mal).
  • Colonial: Drain of wealth, deindustrialization.
  • Post-1947: Green Revolution, 1991 LPG reforms.

Example 12: What triggered 1991 economic reforms?

Balance of Payments crisis. Answer: BoP crisis.

9. Art and Architecture

  • Ancient: Ashokan pillars, Ajanta caves, Sanchi Stupa.
  • Medieval: Chola temples, Mughal (Taj Mahal), Qutub Minar.
  • Colonial: Victoria Memorial, Gateway of India.

Example 13: Who built the Taj Mahal?

Shah Jahan. Answer: Shah Jahan.

10. Women in Indian History

  • Ancient: Gargi, Maitreyi (Vedic scholars).
  • Medieval: Razia Sultana (Delhi Sultanate), Nur Jahan (Mughal).
  • Modern: Sarojini Naidu, Annie Besant, Indira Gandhi.

Example 14: Who was the first woman ruler of Delhi Sultanate?

Razia Sultana. Answer: Razia Sultana.

11. Recent Historical Developments (2024–2025)

  • Archaeology: ASI finds Harappan-era settlement in Rakhigarhi (2024).
  • UNESCO: Hoysala Temples, Moidams of Assam added (2024).
  • Commemorations: 75th Republic Day (2025); Netaji’s 125th birth anniversary (2024).
  • Projects: Ram Mandir inaugurated (Ayodhya, 2024).

Example 15: Which site was added to UNESCO in 2024?

Hoysala Temples. Answer: Hoysala Temples.

14. Historical Sources

  • Ancient: Vedas, Puranas, inscriptions (Ashokan edicts).
  • Medieval: Chronicles (Ain-i-Akbari), travelogues (Ibn Battuta).
  • Modern: British records, newspapers, Gandhi’s writings.

Ain-i-Akbari. Answer: Ain-i-Akbari.

15. Historical Institutions

  • ASI: Archaeological Survey of India (1861); protects 3,600+ monuments.
  • INTACH: Indian National Trust for Art and Cultural Heritage.
  • National Archives: Preserves historical records.

Example 19: Which body protects India’s monuments?

ASI. Answer: ASI.
