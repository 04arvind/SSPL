Time and Distance

1. What is Time and Distance?

Time and distance problems involve calculating speed, distance, or time using their relationship.

2. Basic Formula

  • Speed = Distance / Time.
  • Distance = Speed × Time.
  • Time = Distance / Speed.

3. Unit Conversions

  • 1 km/h = (5/18) m/s.
  • 1 m/s = (18/5) km/h.

4. Relative Speed

  • Same direction: Speed1 – Speed2.
  • Opposite direction: Speed1 + Speed2.

5. Example 1: Basic Calculation

A car travels 120 km at 40 km/h. Find the time.

  • Time = 120 / 40 = 3 hours.
  • Answer: 3 hours.

6. Example 2: Relative Speed

Two trains approach each other at 60 km/h and 40 km/h. When do they meet if initially 200 km apart?

  • Relative speed = 60 + 40 = 100 km/h.
  • Time = 200 / 100 = 2 hours.
  • Answer: 2 hours.

7. Example 3: Conversion

A cyclist travels at 15 km/h. Find speed in m/s.

  • Speed = 15 × (5/18) = 25/6 ≈ 4.17 m/s.
  • Answer: 25/6 m/s.

8. Practice Problems

  1. A bike travels 50 km at 25 km/h. Find the time.
  2. Two cars, 300 km apart, move toward each other at 50 km/h and 70 km/h. When do they meet?
  3. Convert 72 km/h to m/s.
  4. A train covers 180 km in 3 hours. Find its speed.
  5. A man walks 4 km/h. How far does he walk in 2.5 hours?

9. Solutions

  1. Time = 50 / 25 = 2 hours.
  2. Relative speed = 120 km/h; Time = 300 / 120 = 2.5 hours.
  3. 72 × (5/18) = 20 m/s.
  4. Speed = 180 / 3 = 60 km/h.
  5. Distance = 4 × 2.5 = 10 km.

10. Tips

  • Ensure consistent units before calculations.
  • Use relative speed for meeting problems.
  • Practice problems with trains or races.
