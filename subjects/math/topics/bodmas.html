BODMAS (Order of Operations)

1. What is BODMAS?

BODMAS is an acronym that stands for Brackets, Orders (powers/roots), Division, Multiplication, Addition, Subtraction. It defines the correct order in which mathematical operations must be performed to solve an expression accurately. Incorrect application of BODMAS can lead to wrong results, so understanding this rule is fundamental in mathematics.

2. BODMAS Rules

  • Brackets First: Solve expressions inside brackets (parentheses (), curly braces {}, or square brackets []) first.
  • Orders Second: Evaluate powers (e.g., 2²) or roots (e.g., √9) next.
  • Division and Multiplication: Perform these from left to right, as they have equal precedence.
  • Addition and Subtraction: Perform these last, from left to right, as they have equal precedence.

3. Why Use BODMAS?

Without a standard order, expressions like 6 + 2 × 3 could be interpreted differently (e.g., (6 + 2) × 3 = 24 or 6 + (2 × 3) = 12). BODMAS ensures consistency, and the correct answer is 12.

4. Example 1: Simple Expression

Solve: 8 + 4 × 2

  • Step 1: Check for brackets or orders: None.
  • Step 2: Perform multiplication first: 4 × 2 = 8.
  • Step 3: Then addition: 8 + 8 = 16.
  • Answer: 16.

5. Example 2: With Brackets

Solve: (5 + 3) × 2 – 4

  • Step 1: Solve brackets: 5 + 3 = 8.
  • Step 2: Multiply: 8 × 2 = 16.
  • Step 3: Subtract: 16 – 4 = 12.
  • Answer: 12.

6. Common Mistakes

  • Ignoring brackets or solving operations out of order.
  • Treating division as higher priority than multiplication (they are equal).

7. Example 3: Complex Expression

Solve: 12 ÷ 3 × 2 + 4² – (6 – 2)

  • Step 1: Solve brackets: 6 – 2 = 4.
  • Step 2: Evaluate orders: 4² = 16.
  • Step 3: Rewrite: 12 ÷ 3 × 2 + 16 – 4.
  • Step 4: Division and multiplication (left to right): 12 ÷ 3 = 4, then 4 × 2 = 8.
  • Step 5: Rewrite: 8 + 16 – 4.
  • Step 6: Addition and subtraction (left to right): 8 + 16 = 24, then 24 – 4 = 20.
  • Answer: 20.

8. Example 4: Nested Brackets

Solve: {10 – [4 + (8 ÷ 2)]} × 3

  • Step 1: Innermost brackets: 8 ÷ 2 = 4.
  • Step 2: Next brackets: 4 + 4 = 8.
  • Step 3: Curly braces: 10 – 8 = 2.
  • Step 4: Multiply: 2 × 3 = 6.
  • Answer: 6.

9. Practice Problems

  1. Solve: 15 – 3 × 2 + 4
  2. Solve: (7 + 5) ÷ 2 × 3
  3. Solve: 5² – 10 ÷ 2 + 3
  4. Solve: {12 ÷ (4 – 2)} + 5²
  5. Solve: 18 ÷ 3 + 2 × (5 – 3)

10. Solutions to Practice Problems

  1. 15 – 3 × 2 + 4 = 15 – 6 + 4 = 13.
  2. (7 + 5) ÷ 2 × 3 = 12 ÷ 2 × 3 = 6 × 3 = 18.
  3. 5² – 10 ÷ 2 + 3 = 25 – 5 + 3 = 23.
  4. {12 ÷ (4 – 2)} + 5² = {12 ÷ 2} + 25 = 6 + 25 = 31.
  5. 18 ÷ 3 + 2 × (5 – 3) = 6 + 2 × 2 = 6 + 4 = 10.

11. Tips for Mastery

  • Always write down each step to avoid skipping operations.
  • Double-check expressions with nested brackets.
  • Practice with mixed operations to build confidence.
