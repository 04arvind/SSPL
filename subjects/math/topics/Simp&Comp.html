Simple and Compound Interest

1. What is Interest?

Interest is the cost of borrowing money or the return on investment.

2. Simple Interest (SI)

  • Formula: SI = (Principal × Rate × Time) / 100.
  • Amount = Principal + SI.

3. Example 1: Simple Interest

Find SI on 5000 at 8% per year for 3 years.

  • SI = (5000 × 8 × 3) / 100 = 1200.
  • Answer: 1200.

4. Example 2: Find Principal

SI of 600 is earned at 5% per year for 4 years. Find the principal.

  • 600 = (P × 5 × 4) / 100; P = 3000.
  • Answer: 3000.

5. Compound Interest (CI)

  • Formula: Amount = P(1 + R/100)ⁿ; CI = Amount – P.
  • For half-yearly: R = R/2, n = 2n.

6. Example 3: Compound Interest

Find CI on 10000 at 10% per year for 2 years.

  • Amount = 10000 × (1 + 10/100)² = 12100.
  • CI = 12100 – 10000 = 2100.
  • Answer: 2100.

7. Practice Problems

  1. Find SI on 8000 at 6% per year for 5 years.
  2. Find CI on 5000 at 4% per year for 2 years.
  3. SI of 900 is earned at 3% per year for 5 years. Find the principal.
  4. Find the amount for 2000 at 5% CI for 3 years.
  5. Compare SI and CI on 10000 at 10% for 2 years.

8. Solutions

  1. SI = (8000 × 6 × 5) / 100 = 2400.
  2. CI = 5408 – 5000 = 408.
  3. P = (900 × 100) / 15 = 6000.
  4. Amount = 2000 × (1 + 5/100)³ = 2315.25.
  5. SI = 2000; CI = 2100; CI > SI by 100.

9. Tips

  • Use SI for linear growth, CI for exponential.
  • Check time units (years, months).
  • Practice CI with different compounding frequencies.
