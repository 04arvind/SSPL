Pipes & Cisterns

1. What are Pipes & Cisterns?

Pipes and cisterns problems calculate the time to fill or empty a tank based on inlet and outlet pipe rates.

2. Key Concepts

  • Inlet: Rate = 1/a per hour.
  • Outlet: Rate = 1/b per hour.
  • Combined: (1/a) – (1/b).

3. Example 1: Single Inlet

A pipe fills a tank in 6 hours. How long to fill 1/3 of the tank?

  • Rate = 1/6 per hour.
  • Time = (1/3) × 6 = 2 hours.
  • Answer: 2 hours.

4. Example 2: Combined Pipes

A fills in 10 hours, B in 15 hours. How long together?

  • Rate = (1/10) + (1/15) = 1/6.
  • Time = 6 hours.
  • Answer:6 hours.

5. Example 3: Inlet + Outlet

A fills in 12 hours, B empties in 8 hours. How long to fill?

  • Rate = (1/12 – 1/8) = –1/24.
  • Answer: Tank never fills.

6. Practice Problems

  1. A pipe fills a tank in 8 hours. How long for 1/4 of it?
  2. Two pipes fill a tank in 12 and 16 hours. How long together?
  3. A fills in 10 hours, B empties in 15 hours. How long with both?
  4. A and B fill a tank in 6 hours; A alone in 10 hours. Find B’s time.
  5. A fills in 20 hours, B and C empty in 30 and 40 hours. How long with all open?

7. Solutions

  1. 2 hours.
  2. ≈ 7 hours.
  3. 30 hours.
  4. 15 hours.
  5. Tank empties.

8. Tips

  • Convert to rates (1/time).
  • Check if tank fills or empties.
  • Practice with multiple pipes.
