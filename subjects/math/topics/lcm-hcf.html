LCM and HCF

1. What are LCM and HCF?

  • LCM (Least Common Multiple): The smallest number that is exactly divisible by each of the given numbers. Used in problems related to repetition, common events, or scheduling.

  • HCF (Highest Common Factor): The largest number that divides all the given numbers without leaving any remainder. Used in simplifying ratios, sharing quantities, etc.

  • Example: For 6 and 8:
    • LCM = 24
    • HCF = 2

2. Methods to Find HCF

  • Method 1: Prime Factorization
    • Step 1: Write prime factors of each number.
    • Step 2: Take only the common factors with the smallest power.
    • Example: HCF(24, 36)
      • 24 = 2×2×2×3 = 2³×3
      • 36 = 2×2×3×3 = 2²×3²
      • Common = 2²×3 = 12
  • Method 2: Euclidean Algorithm
    • Keep dividing the larger number by the smaller number until the remainder is 0. The divisor at this step is the HCF.
    • Example: HCF(48, 18)
      • 48 ÷ 18 = 2, remainder = 12
      • 18 ÷ 12 = 1, remainder = 6
      • 12 ÷ 6 = 2, remainder = 0
      • → HCF = 6

3. Methods to Find LCM

  • Method 1: Prime Factorization
    • Step 1: Write prime factors of each number.
    • Step 2: Take all factors with the highest power.
    • Example: LCM(12, 15)
      • 12 = 2²×3
      • 15 = 3×5
      • LCM = 2²×3×5 = 60
  • Method 2: LCM Formula
    • LCM(a, b) = (a × b) ÷ HCF(a, b)
    • Example: a = 12, b = 18
    • HCF = 6 → LCM = (12×18) / 6 = 216 / 6 = 36

4. Example 1: HCF by Factorization

Find HCF of 12 and 18.

  • 12 = 2² × 3; 18 = 2 × 3².
  • HCF = 2 × 3 = 6.
  • Answer: 6.

5. Example 2: LCM by Formula

Find LCM of 8 and 12.

  • HCF(8, 12) = 4 (by factorization: 8 = 2³, 12 = 2² × 3).
  • LCM = (8 × 12) / 4 = 24.
  • Answer 24.

6. Example 3: Euclidean Algorithm

Find HCF of 48 and 18.

  • 48 ÷ 18 = 2 remainder 12.
  • 18 ÷ 6 = 3 remainder 0.
  • HCF = 6.
  • Answer 6.

7. Example 4: Application

Two bells ring every 6 and 8 minutes. When do they ring together?

  • LCM(6, 8) = (6 × 8) / HCF(6, 8) = 48 / 2 = 24.
  • Answer: After 24 minutes.

8. Practice Problems

  1. Find HCF of 24 and 36.
  2. Find LCM of 15 and 20.
  3. Find HCF of 27 and 45 using Euclidean algorithm.
  4. Two numbers have HCF 6 and LCM 72. Find one possible pair.
  5. Three ropes of lengths 4, 6, 8 m. Find minimum length to cut equal pieces.

9. Solutions

  1. HCF(24, 36) = 12 (24 = 2³ × 3, 36 = 2² × 3²).
  2. LCM(15, 20) = 60 (15 × 20 / 5).
  3. 45 ÷ 27 = 18, 27 ÷ 18 = 9, 18 ÷ 9 = 0; HCF = 9.
  4. Possible pair: 18, 24 (HCF = 6, LCM = 72).
  5. LCM(4, 6, 8) = 24 m.

10. Tips

  • Use Euclidean algorithm for large numbers.
  • Verify LCM/HCF using formula.
  • Apply to real-world scenarios like time or ratios.
