Decimals and Fractions

1. Definitions

  • Fraction: A number that represents a part of a whole. It has two parts: the numerator (top) and the denominator (bottom). Example: 3/4 means 3 parts out of 4.
  • Decimal: A number expressed using a decimal point. It shows part of a whole using place values like tenths, hundredths, etc. Example: 0.75.
  • Terminating Decimal: A decimal that ends or terminates after a certain number of digits. Example: 0.25.
  • Non-Terminating Repeating Decimal: A decimal that repeats a pattern forever. Example: 0.333....

2. Converting Fractions to Decimals

To convert a fraction into a decimal, divide the numerator by the denominator.

  • Example 1: Convert 3/4 to decimal → 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75
  • Example 2: Convert 1/2 → 1 ÷ 2 = 0.5
  • Example 3: Convert 1/3 → 1 ÷ 3 = 0.333… (Repeating Decimal)

Tip: If the denominator has only 2 and/or 5 as prime factors, the decimal will terminate.

3. Converting Decimals to Fractions

Write the decimal as a fraction and simplify it.

  • Terminating Decimal:
    Example: 0.6 = 6/10 = 3/5
  • Repeating Decimal: Use algebraic method:
    Example: Convert 0.666… to fraction:
    Let x = 0.666…
    10x = 6.666…
    Now subtract: 10x - x = 6.666… - 0.666…
    9x = 6 → x = 6/9 = 2/3

4. Comparing Fractions and Decimals

Convert either all to decimals or all to fractions, then compare.

  • Example: Compare 3/4 and 0.7 → 3/4 = 0.75, so 0.75 > 0.7
  • Tip: Use LCM if converting decimals to fractions with unlike denominators.

5. Word Problems

Understanding application-based questions is important in exams:

  • Q: A person spends 0.6 of his salary on rent. What fraction of his salary is spent on rent?
    Ans: 0.6 = 6/10 = 3/5
  • Q: Which is greater: 7/10 or 0.65?
    Ans: 7/10 = 0.7, and 0.7 > 0.65

6. Example 1: Fraction to Decimal

Convert 5/8 to decimal:

  • 5 ÷ 8 = 0.625.
  • Answer: 625.

7. Example 2: Decimal to Fraction

Convert 0.4 to fraction:

  • 0.4 = 4/10 = 2/5.
  • Answer: 2/5.

8. Operations on Fractions

  • Addition: (a/b) + (c/d) = (ad + cb)/(bd).
  • Multiplication: (a/b) × (c/d) = (a×c)/(b×d).
  • Division: (a/b) ÷ (c/d) = (a/b) × (d/c).

9. Example 3: Fraction Addition

Solve: (2/3) + (1/4)

  • LCM of 3, 4 = 12.
  • (2/3) = (8/12), (1/4) = (3/12).
  • (8/12) + (3/12) = 11/12.
  • Answer: 11/12.

10. Example 4: Decimal Multiplication

Solve: 0.3 × 0.6

  • 0.3 × 0.6 = 3/10 × 6/10 = 18/100 = 0.18.
  • Answer: 0.18.

11. Practice Problems

  1. Convert 7/20 to decimal.
  2. Convert 0.125 to fraction.
  3. Solve: (5/6) + (2/3).
  4. Solve: 0.4 × 0.25.
  5. Solve: (3/4) ÷ (5/3).

12. Solutions

  1. 7/20 = 7 ÷ 20 = 0.35.
  2. 0.125 = 125/1000 = 1/8.
  3. (5/6) + (4/6) = 9/6 = 3/2.
  4. 0.4 × 0.25 = 0.1.
  5. (3/4) × (3/5) = 15/20 = 3/4.

13. Tips

  • Simplify fractions to lowest terms.
  • Check decimal conversions by reversing.
  • Practice repeating decimal problems.
