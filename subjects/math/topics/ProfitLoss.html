Profit and Loss

1. What is Profit and Loss?

Profit and Loss deals with calculating gains or losses in transactions, typically in buying and selling goods.

2. Key Terms

  • Cost Price (CP): Price at which an item is bought.
  • Selling Price (SP): Price at which an item is sold.
  • Profit: SP > CP; Profit = SP – CP.
  • Loss: SP < CP; Loss = CP – SP.
  • Profit % = (Profit / CP) × 100.
  • Loss % = (Loss / CP) × 100.

3. Formulas

  • SP = CP + Profit or CP – Loss.
  • CP = (100 × SP) / (100 + Profit%) or (100 × SP) / (100 – Loss%).

4. Example 1: Calculate Profit

A book is bought for 200 and sold for 250. Find profit and profit %.

  • Profit = 250 – 200 = 50.
  • Profit % = (50 / 200) × 100 = 25%.
  • Answer: Profit = 50, Profit % = 25%.

5. Example 2: Find Selling Price

A shirt’s CP is 300, and a 20% profit is desired. Find SP.

  • SP = (100 + 20) × 300 / 100 = 360.
  • Answer: SP = 360.

6. Example 3: Find Cost Price

A watch is sold for 400 with a 25% loss. Find CP.

  • CP = (100 × 400) / (100 – 25) = 533.33.
  • Answer: CP = 533.33.

7. Example 4: Discount

A laptop’s marked price is 50000, sold at a 10% discount with 20% profit. Find CP.

  • SP = 50000 × (100 – 10) / 100 = 45000.
  • CP = (100 × 45000) / (100 + 20) = 37500.
  • Answer: CP = 37500.

8. Practice Problems

  1. A pen is bought for 50 and sold for 60. Find profit %.
  2. A phone’s CP is 8000, sold at 10% loss. Find SP.
  3. A table is sold for 1200 with 20% profit. Find CP.
  4. A shopkeeper sells an item for 900 with 25% loss. Find CP.
  5. A bike’s marked price is 40000, sold at 15% discount with 10% profit. Find CP.

9. Solutions

  1. Profit = 10; Profit % = 20%.
  2. SP = (100 – 10) × 8000 / 100 = 7200.
  3. CP = (100 × 1200) / (100 + 20) = 1000.
  4. CP = (100 × 900) / (100 – 25) = 1200.
  5. SP = 40000 × (100 – 15) / 100 = 34000; CP = 30909.09.

10. Tips

  • Clearly identify CP and SP.
  • Use percentage formulas for quick calculations.
  • Check discounts by reversing calculations.
