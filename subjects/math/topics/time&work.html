Time and Work

1. What is Time and Work?

Time and work problems calculate how long it takes for individuals or groups to complete tasks, based on their work rates.

2. Basic Concept

  • Work rate = Work done / Time taken.
  • If A completes a job in n days, A’s 1-day work = 1/n.

3. Combined Work Formula

  • If A does a job in a days and B in b days, combined work = (1/a) + (1/b).
  • Time together = (a × b) / (a + b).

4. Example 1: Individual Work

A can do a job in 6 days. How much work does A do in 2 days?

  • A’s 1-day work = 1/6.
  • Work in 2 days = 2 × (1/6) = 1/3.
  • Answer: 1/3 of the job.

5. Example 2: Combined Work

A does a job in 10 days, B in 15 days. How long do they take together?

  • A’s 1-day work = 1/10; B’s = 1/15.
  • Combined work = (1/10) + (1/15) = 1/6.
  • Time together = 6 days.
  • Answer: 6 days.

6. Example 3: Partial Work

A can do a job in 12 days, B in 18 days. They work together for 4 days, then A leaves. How long does B take to finish?

  • Combined 1-day work = (1/12) + (1/18) = 5/36.
  • Work in 4 days = 4 × (5/36) = 5/9.
  • Remaining work = 1 – 5/9 = 4/9.
  • B’s time = (4/9) / (1/18) = 8 days.
  • Answer: 8 days.

7. Practice Problems

  1. A completes a job in 8 days. How much work is done in 3 days?
  2. B can do a job in 20 days, C in 30 days. How long do they take together?
  3. A and B together can do a job in 12 days. A alone takes 18 days. How long does B take alone?
  4. A can do a job in 15 days. After 5 days, B joins and they finish in 6 more days. How long does B take alone?
  5. A, B, C can do a job in 10, 15, 20 days. How long do they take together?

8. Solutions

  1. Work = 3 × (1/8) = 3/8.
  2. Combined work = (1/20) + (1/30) = 1/12; Time = 12 days.
  3. B’s work = 1/12 – 1/18 = 1/36; B alone = 36 days.
  4. B alone = 22.5 days.
  5. Combined work = (1/10) + (1/15) + (1/20) = 13/60; Time = 60/13 days.

9. Tips

  • Focus on 1-day work rates.
  • Use LCM for combined work calculations.
  • Check partial work by summing contributions.
