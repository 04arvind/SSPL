Age Calculations

1. What are Age Calculations?

Age calculation problems involve finding the ages of people based on relationships between their ages now, in the past, or in the future.

2. Key Approach

  • Represent ages using variables (e.g., let present age = x).
  • Form equations based on given conditions.
  • Solve linear or simultaneous equations.

3. Example 1: Simple Age Problem

A father is 3 times as old as his son. If the father is 36 years old, find the son’s age.

  • Let son’s age = x.
  • Father’s age = 3x = 36.
  • x = 36 / 3 = 12.
  • Answer: Son’s age = 12 years.

4. Example 2: Past Ages

Five years ago, a mother was 4 times as old as her daughter. Now, the mother is 3 times as old. Find their present ages.

  • Let daughter’s present age = x, mother’s = y.
  • Five years ago: (y – 5) = 4(x – 5).
  • Now: y = 3x.
  • Solve: x = 15, y = 45.
  • Answer: Daughter = 15 years, Mother = 45 years.

5. Example 3: Future Ages

The sum of the ages of A and B is 50 years. In 10 years, A will be twice as old as B. Find their present ages.

  • Let A’s age = x, B’s = y.
  • x + y = 50.
  • In 10 years: (x + 10) = 2(y + 10).
  • Solve: x ≈ 36.67, y ≈ 13.33.
  • Answer: A ≈ 36.67 years, B ≈ 13.33 years.

6. Practice Problems

  1. A man is 5 times as old as his son, who is 8 years old. Find the man’s age.
  2. Two years ago, a father was 6 times as old as his son. Now, he is 5 times as old. Find their present ages.
  3. The sum of ages of X and Y is 60. Ten years ago, X was twice as old as Y. Find their ages.
  4. In 5 years, a mother will be 3 times as old as her son. Their age difference is 20 years. Find their ages.
  5. A’s age is twice B’s. In 8 years, A will be 1.5 times B’s age. Find their ages.

7. Solutions

  1. Man’s age = 40 years.
  2. Son = 12, Father = 60.
  3. X = 40, Y = 20.
  4. Son = 10, Mother = 30.
  5. A = 16, B = 8.

8. Tips

  • Define variables clearly.
  • Check solutions by verifying past/future conditions.
  • Practice problems with fractional ages for accuracy.
