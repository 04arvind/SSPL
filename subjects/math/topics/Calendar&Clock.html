Calendar

1. What is Calendar?

Calendar problems involve finding the day of the week for a given date or calculating intervals between dates.

2. Key Concepts

  • Ordinary Year: 365 days = 1 odd day.
  • Leap Year: 366 days = 2 odd days, divisible by 4 (except centuries, by 400).
  • Day Cycle: Days repeat every 7 days.

3. Counting Odd Days

  • 1 ordinary year = 1 odd day.
  • 1 leap year = 2 odd days.
  • 100 years = 5 odd days.

4. Example 1: Day of Week

Find the day on 15 August 1947 if 1 January 1900 was Monday.

  • Years 1900–1946: 12 leap, 35 ordinary = 59 odd days = 3 days.
  • 1 Jan 1947 = Thursday; 1 Jan to 15 Aug = 226 days = 2 odd days.
  • Thursday + 2 = Saturday.
  • Answer: Saturday.

5. Example 2: Same Day

On what date in 2025 will the day be the same as 15 August 1947 (Saturday)?

  • 1947–2024: 19 leap, 58 ordinary = 96 odd days = 5 days.
  • 15 Aug 2024 = Thursday; 2025 adds 1 odd day = Friday.
  • Friday + 1 = 16 Aug 2025.
  • Answer: 16 August 2025.

6. Practice Problems

  1. If 1 January 2000 was Saturday, find the day on 1 January 2001.
  2. Find the day on 26 January 1950 if 1 January 1900 was Monday.
  3. How many days between 15 August 2023 and 15 August 2024?
  4. Find the day on 31 December 2025 if 2025 is not a leap year.
  5. January 2025 will be a Wednesday, on what date will it be the same day again in 2026?

7. Solutions

  1. 2000 is leap; 1 odd day; Monday.
  3. January 1950 = Friday; Jan 26 = Tuesday.
  5. 366 days (2024 is leap).
  6. 1 Jan 2025 = Wednesday; 1 Jan 1 odd day = Thursday.
  8. 1 Jan 2026 = Friday.

8. Tips

  • Memorize leap year rules.
  • Break dates into years + months + months + days.
  • Check known dates.


Clock

1. What are Clock?

Clock problems calculate angles between clock hands or time intervals for hands to align.

2. Key Concepts

  • Hour Hand: 6° per minute.
  • Minute Hand: 6° per minute.
  • Relatively Speed: 5.5° per minute.
  • Coinciding: Every 12/11 minutes.

3. Example 1: Angle Between Hands

Find angle at 3:00 p.m.

  • Hour hand: 3 × 30 = 90°.
  • Minute hand: 0°.
  • Angle: |90 – 0| = 90°.
  • Answer: 90°.

4. Example 2: Coinciding

At what time after 2:00 do hands coincide?

  • Hour hand: 2 × 30 = 60°.
  • 5.5 × M = 60; M ≈ 10.9 min.
  • Answer: ≈ 2:11.

5. Example 3: Opposite Angle

At what time after 4:00 are hands at 180°?

  • |120 – 5.5t| = 180; t ≈ 21.82.
  • Answer: ≈ 4:22.

6. Practice Problems

  1. Find angle at 6:00.
  2. Find time after 5:00 when hands coincide.
  3. Find time after 3:00 when hands are at 90°.
  4. How many times do hands coincide from 12:00 to 12:00 PM?
  5. Find time after 7:00 when hands are at 180°.

7. Solutions

  1. 180°.
  2. ≈ 5:27.
  3. 0 or ≈ 3:27.
  4. 11 times.
  5. ≈ 7:30 or 7:44.

8. Tips

  • Use absolute value for smaller angle.
  • Check coinciding times by counting.
  • Practice with right and straight angles.
