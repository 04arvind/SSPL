Percentages

1. What is a Percentage?

Percentage is a way to express a number as a fraction of 100, denoted by %. For example, 25% means 25/100 or 0.25.

2. Converting Percentages

  • To decimal: Divide by 100 (e.g., 40% = 0.4).
  • To fraction: Write as /100 and simplify (e.g., 75% = 3/4).
  • From decimal: Multiply by 100 (e.g., 0.6 = 60%).
  • From fraction: Convert to decimal, multiply by 100 (e.g., 1/4 = 25%).

3. Percentage Formula

  • Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100.
  • Part = (Percentage × Whole) / 100.
  • Whole = (Part × 100) / Percentage.

4. Example 1: Find Percentage

What is 30% of 200?

  • Part = (30 × 200) / 100 = 60.
  • Answer: 60.

5. Example 2: Find Whole

If 15% of a number is 45, find the number.

  • Whole = (45 × 100) / 15 = 300.
  • Answer: 300.

6. Example 3: Percentage Increase

A price increases from 100 to 120. Find the percentage increase.

  • Increase = 120 – 100 = 20.
  • Percentage increase = (20 / 100) × 100 = 20%.
  • Answer: 20%.

7. Example 4: Percentage Decrease

A shirt’s price drops from 500 to 400. Find the percentage decrease.

  • Decrease = 500 – 400 = 100.
  • Percentage decrease = (100 / 500) × 100 = 20%.
  • Answer: 20%.

8. Practice Problems

  1. Find 25% of 80.
  2. If 40% of a number is 24, find the number.
  3. A salary increases from 2000 to 2500. Find the percentage increase.
  4. A product’s price drops from 300 to 240. Find the percentage decrease.
  5. 60 out of 150 students passed. Find the pass percentage.

9. Solutions

  1. (25 × 80) / 100 = 20.
  2. (24 × 100) / 40 = 60.
  3. Increase = 500; (500 / 2000) × 100 = 25%.
  4. Decrease = 60; (60 / 300) × 100 = 20%.
  5. (60 / 150) × 100 = 40%.

10. Tips

  • Convert percentages to decimals for easier calculations.
  • Double-check percentage increase/decrease by reversing.
  • Practice real-world problems like discounts and taxes.
