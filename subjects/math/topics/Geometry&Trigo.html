Geometry and Trigonometry

1. What is Geometry?

Geometry is a branch of mathematics concerned with the study of shapes, sizes, angles, dimensions, and positions of objects. It explores the properties and relationships of points, lines, surfaces, and solids. In competitive exams, geometry questions commonly involve 2D figures (like triangles, circles, quadrilaterals) and their angles, areas, and perimeters.

  • Plane Geometry: Deals with 2D shapes such as lines, circles, and polygons.
  • Solid Geometry: Involves 3D shapes like cubes, cylinders, spheres, etc.
  • Coordinate Geometry: Uses algebra and a coordinate plane to study geometric figures.

2. Basic Concepts

  • Triangle: A 3-sided polygon. Types include scalene, isosceles, and equilateral.
    • Sum of interior angles is always 180°.
    • Angle Sum Property: ∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°
    • Exterior Angle Theorem: Exterior angle = sum of opposite interior angles.
  • Pythagoras Theorem: Applies to right-angled triangles.
    • Formula: a² + b² = c², where c is the hypotenuse (longest side opposite right angle).
    • Useful in finding missing sides in 90° triangles, especially in coordinate geometry and mensuration.
  • Circle: A round figure with all points equidistant from the center.
    • Radius: Distance from center to any point on the circle.
    • Diameter: Twice the radius; longest chord.
    • Chord: A line joining any two points on the circle.
    • Arc: A part of the circumference.
    • Central Angle: Angle subtended by an arc at the center.
  • Congruence: Two figures (especially triangles) are congruent if they are exactly the same in shape and size.
    • SAS: Side-Angle-Side
    • ASA: Angle-Side-Angle
    • SSS: Side-Side-Side
    • RHS: Right angle-Hypotenuse-Side (for right-angled triangles)

3. Example 1: Pythagoras Theorem

In a right triangle, sides are 3 cm, 4 cm. Find hypotenuse.

  • Hypotenuse² = 3² + 4² = 25.
  • Hypotenuse = √25 = 5 cm.
  • Answer: 5 cm.

4. Trigonometry Basics

Trigonometry is the study of relationships between angles and sides of right-angled triangles. It is widely used in geometry, physics, navigation, and competitive exams.

  • Trigonometric Ratios: Defined for a right-angled triangle.
    • sin θ = Opposite side / Hypotenuse
    • cos θ = Adjacent side / Hypotenuse
    • tan θ = Opposite side / Adjacent side
    • cot θ = 1 / tan θ = Adjacent / Opposite
    • sec θ = 1 / cos θ = Hypotenuse / Adjacent
    • cosec θ = 1 / sin θ = Hypotenuse / Opposite
  • Standard Angles and Their Trigonometric Values:
    Angle sin θ cos θ tan θ
    0°010
    30°1/2√3/21/√3
    45°1/√21/√21
    60°√3/21/2√3
    90°10Undefined
  • Identity 1: sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  • Identity 2: 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
  • Identity 3: 1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ
  • Identity 4: tanθ = sinθ / cosθ
  • Identity 5: cotθ = cosθ / sinθ
  • Identity 6: sin(90° – θ) = cosθ
  • Identity 7: cos(90° – θ) = sinθ
  • Identity 8: tan(90° – θ) = cotθ
  • Identity 9: cot(90° – θ) = tanθ
  • Identity 10: sec(90° – θ) = cosecθ
  • Identity 11: cosec(90° – θ) = secθ

5. Example 1: Using Trigonometric Ratios

Problem: In a right-angled triangle, the side opposite to angle θ is 3 cm, the adjacent side is 4 cm. Find sin θ, cos θ, and tan θ.

  • Use Pythagoras theorem to find hypotenuse:
    Hypotenuse² = 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25 ⇒ Hypotenuse = 5 cm
  • sin θ = Opposite / Hypotenuse = 3 / 5 = 0.6
  • cos θ = Adjacent / Hypotenuse = 4 / 5 = 0.8
  • tan θ = Opposite / Adjacent = 3 / 4 = 0.75
  • Answer: sin θ = 0.6, cos θ = 0.8, tan θ = 0.75

6. Example 2: Trigonometry

In a right triangle, angle = 30°, hypotenuse = 10 cm. Find opposite side.

  • sin 30° = Opposite / 10; 1/2 = Opposite / 10.
  • Opposite = 5 cm.
  • Answer: 5 cm.

7. Example 3: Height and Distance Application

Problem: A ladder 10 meters long is leaning against a wall. It makes an angle of 60° with the ground. Find the height of the wall (vertical height reached by the ladder).

  • Use the trigonometric ratio:
    sin θ = Opposite / Hypotenuse
  • Here, θ = 60°, Hypotenuse = 10 m, Opposite = height of the wall (let it be h)
  • sin 60° = h / 10
  • √3/2 = h / 10
  • h = 10 × √3 / 2 = 5√3 ≈ 8.66 m
  • Answer: The height of the wall is approximately 8.66 meters.

8. Example 4: Triangle Area

Find area of a triangle with vertices (0,0), (3,0), (0,4).

  • Base = 3, Height = 4.
  • Area = (1/2) × 3 × 4 = 6 square units.
  • Answer: 6 square units.

8. Practice Problems

  1. Find the hypotenuse of a right triangle with legs 6 cm, 8 cm.
  2. Find sin θ if opposite = 5 cm, hypotenuse = 13 cm.
  3. Find area of a triangle with base 10 cm, height 5 cm.
  4. In a circle, radius = 7 cm. Find area (use π = 22/7).
  5. If tan θ = 3/4, find cos θ.

8. Solutions

  1. Hypotenuse = 10 cm.
  2. sin θ = 5 / 13.
  3. Area = 25 cm².
  4. Area = 154 cm².
  5. cos θ = 4/5.

9. Tips

  • Memorize trigonometric ratios and values.
  • Use Pythagoras for right triangles.
  • Practice coordinate geometry for area calculations.
